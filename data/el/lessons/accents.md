# The Accent Mark (Ο Τόνος)

In Modern Greek, the accent mark (ο τόνος) is not optional. It is a crucial compass that tells you exactly how to pronounce a word. In English, you have to guess where the stress goes (e.g. "**re**cord" vs "re**cord**"). In Greek, the language hands you the answer on a silver platter.

---

## 1. The Fundamental Rules

There are only three rules you need to know about accents:

1. **The Target**: The accent mark **only** goes on top of vowels (α, ε, η, ι, υ, ο, ω). Never on a consonant!
2. **The Limit**: It can only be placed on one of the **last three syllables** of a word. You will never see an accent mark at the very beginning of a long word.
3. **The Monosyllable Rule**: Words with only one syllable (e.g., ναι = yes, όχι = no - wait, `όχι` has two syllables!) Words like **και** (and), **με** (with), **το** (the) generally do *not* take an accent mark. 

> [!TIP]
> **Exceptions to the Monosyllable Rule**
> There are exactly two one-syllable words that *do* take an accent mark, just to distinguish them from identical words:
> - **πού** means "where?", while **που** means "that/which".
> - **πώς** means "how?", while **πως** means "that".

---

## 2. Double Vowels & The Accent

What happens when a word has a double vowel (like **ει** or **ου**)? Which letter gets the accent mark?

If the combination makes a single sound (like the `[ee]` in **εί**ναι), the accent mark always goes on the **second** letter.

- **εί**ναι (he is)
- αυτ**οί** (they)
- **ού**τε (neither)

If you see an accent mark on the *first* letter of a combo, it means the combo is broken! You must pronounce both vowels separately.
- μ**άι**ος (Ma-yos = May)
- π**λάι** (pla-i = beside)

---

## 3. Why Accents Matter

The placement of the accent isn't just about sounding correct; it can change the entire meaning of a word! English has this too (e.g., "I will **de**sert the **de**ssert"). 

Look at these "False Twins":

::: content-card
**νóμος** *(nó-mos)* = law  
**νομóς** *(no-mós)* = prefecture/district
::: end

::: content-card
**γέρος** *(yé-ros)* = old man  
**γερός** *(ye-rós)* = strong
::: end

::: content-card
**πότε** *(pó-te)* = when?  
**ποτέ** *(po-té)* = never
::: end

If you say "never" when you meant to say "when?", you're going to have a very confusing conversation!

---

## 🎯 Quick Review

1. Hit the accented syllable with your voice!
2. If a word has 2+ syllables, it *must* have an accent mark.
3. If a word has 1 syllable, it almost *never* has an accent mark (with the exceptions of **πού** and **πώς**).
4. For double vowels (**ει, οι, ου**), the accent goes on the **second** letter.
