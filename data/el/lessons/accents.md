# Accents (Τονισμός)
In Modern Greek, the accent mark (ο τόνος) is a crucial orthographic feature that indicates which syllable of a word is stressed. Unlike English, where stress patterns can be ambiguous, Greek uses a visible mark to ensure correct pronunciation.

## The Fundamental Rules

- **Target:** The accent goes **only** on top of vowels.
- **Position:** Only the last three syllables of a word can carry an accent.
- **Monosyllables:** Words with only one syllable generally do not take an accent (with a few grammatical exceptions).

## Why Accents Matter

The placement of the accent can change the entire meaning of a word. For example, νόμος (nómos) means "law", while νομός (nomós) means "prefecture" or "district".
