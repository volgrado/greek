# Unit 25: Adjective Comparison (Better, Faster)

In Greek, there are two ways to compare things (e.g., "tall" ➔ "taller"). You can use the easy "more" method or the specialized "suffix" method.

---

## 1. The Easy Way: Πιο + Adjective
This is currently the most common way to speak. Just put the word **πιο** (more) in front of the adjective.

- **Μεγάλος** (Big) ➔ **Πιο μεγάλος** (Bigger)
- **Ωραίος** (Beautiful) ➔ **Πιο ωραίος** (More beautiful)

To say "than," use the word **από**.
- Ο Γιώργος είναι **πιο ψηλός από** τον Νίκο. (George is taller than Nikos).

---

## 2. The Native Way: The -τερος Suffix
If you want to sound more traditional or formal, you add the suffix **-τερος, -τερη, -τερο** to the stem of the adjective.

- **Ψηλός** ➔ **Ψηλότερος** (Taller)
- **Νέος** (Young) ➔ **Νεότερος** (Younger)
- **Ωραίος** ➔ **Ωραιότερος** (More beautiful)

---

## 3. Superlatives (The Best)
To say "the most" or "the best," you simply add the **Definite Article** (ο, η, το) in front of your comparison.

- **Ο πιο καλός** (The best / The most good)
- **Ο καλύτερος** (The better one / The best one)

---

## 4. The "Very" Superlative: -τατος
If you want to say something is "extremely" or "very, very" X, use the ancient suffix **-τατος**.
- **Ωραιότατος** (Extremely beautiful)
- **Φθηνότατος** (Super cheap)

---

## 5. The Top 3 Irregulars
These three are used in every sentence. You must learn their "native" comparison forms:

1. **Καλός** (Good) ➔ **Καλύτερος** (Better)
2. **Κακός** (Bad) ➔ **Χειρότερος** (Worse)
3. **Πολύς** (Much) ➔ **Περισσότερος** (More)
