# The Adjectives
In Greek, adjectives must agree with the noun they modify in **gender**, **number**, and **case**. This means that a single adjective like "good" can have many different forms.

## Gender Agreement

Every adjective has three genders to match whatever noun it describes.

::: adjective-showcase mt-8
Ο καλ **ός** πατέρας The good father (Masc)Η καλ **ή** μητέρα The good mother (Fem)Το καλ **ό** παιδί The good child (Neut)
::: end

## Common Adjective Patterns

Most adjectives follow the **-ος, -η, -ο** pattern or the **-ος, -α, -ο** pattern.

| Root | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|---|---|---|---|
| Good | καλός | καλή | καλό |
| Tall | ψηλός | ψηλή | ψηλό |
| Small | μικρός | μικρή | μικρό |
| Beautiful | ωραίος | ωραία | ωραίο |

::: info-box mt-12

### Declension Rule

Adjectives are declined exactly like the nouns of the same gender. A masculine adjective in *-ος* declines exactly like a masculine noun in *-ος*.

::: end
