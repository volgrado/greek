# Ancient Remnants in Modern Greek

Welcome to Level C2: Mastery.

Modern Greek is a living, breathing language, but it carries the ghosts of its 3,000-year history everywhere it goes. 

In the 19th and 20th centuries, Greece had two official languages: 
1. **Demotic (Δημοτική):** The natural spoken language of the people. 
2. **Katharevousa (Καθαρεύουσα):** An artificial, highly purified, "ancient-sounding" language used by the government, schools, and news anchors.

In 1976, Demotic officially won and became the sole language of Greece. However, the 150 years of Katharevousa left deep scars on the modern language. 

To achieve true C2 fluency, you must understand the "Ancient Remnants" that are still used in the news, literature, and formal speech today.

## 1. Dative Expressions (The Lost Case)
Ancient Greek had a 5th case: The **Dative Case** (used to show "to" or "for" someone). Modern Greek replaced this entirely by using "σε + Accusative". 

However, dozens of Dative phrases survived as frozen, highly formal expressions. You must memorize them as whole blocks:
*   **Εν τω μεταξύ** (In the meantime)
*   **Δόξα τω Θεώ** (Glory to God / Thank God)
*   **Εν πάση περιπτώσει** (In any case / Anyway)
*   **Εν τέλει** (In the end / Finally)
*   **Τοις μετρητοίς** (In cash)

> [!NOTE]
> The tiny word **εν** (in) is an ancient preposition that ALWAYS triggers the Dative case. You will see it constantly in C2 vocabulary!

## 2. Ancient Prepositions
C2 Greek resurrects several ancient prepositions that demand the **Genitive case**. They are used in formal contexts, journalism, and academic writing.

| Ancient Preposition | Meaning | Modern Equivalent | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ενώπιον** (+ Gen) | Before / In the presence of | Μπροστά σε | Ορκίστηκε ενώπιον του δικαστηρίου. |
| **Λόγω** (+ Gen) | Due to / Because of | Επειδή / Εξαιτίας | Κλειστό λόγω βροχής. |
| **Μέσω** (+ Gen) | Via / Through | Από | Έφτασα μέσω Αθηνών. |
| **Άνευ** (+ Gen) | Without | Χωρίς | Διασταύρωση άνευ σηματοδότη. |
| **Κατά** (+ Gen) | Against | Εναντίον | Διαδήλωση κατά της κυβέρνησης. |

## 3. The 3rd Declension Nouns (Singular -εως / Plural -εις)
Katharevousa dragged a group of ancient "3rd Declension" nouns into modern Greek. These are almost always abstract feminine nouns ending in **-η** (ancient **-ις**). You will recognize them because they almost all end in **-ση, -ξη, or -ψη**!

Their defining feature is their formal Genitive singular ending: **-εως**.
*   **Η πόλη** (The city) ➔ Το κέντρο **της πόλεως** (The center of the city).
*   **Η απόφαση** (The decision) ➔ Η λήψη **της αποφάσεως** (The taking of the decision).

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **The Accent Shift Rule:**  
> If the word has more than two syllables (like *κυβέρνηση*), the stress **must move one syllable forward** in the formal *-εως* genitive (➔ **κυβερνήσεως**). If it has only two syllables (like *πόλη*), the stress stays in the same place (**πόλεως**).

**The Plural (-εις):**  
In the **plural**, these nouns ALWAYS use the ancient endings: Nominative/Accusative **-εις** and Genitive **-εων**.
*   Οι **αποφάσεις** (The decisions) ➔ Των **αποφάσεων**.
*   Οι **πόλεις** (The cities) ➔ Των **πόλεων**.

## 4. Bonus: C2 Adjectives & Participles
To truly master formal Greek, you must recognize two ancient adjective classes that survive perfectly intact:

**The -ης / -ες Adjectives:**  
These do not have separate forms for masculine and feminine. In the Genitive singular (for all genders), they take the ancient ending **-ους**.
*   **ακριβής** (exact/precise), **διεθνής** (international), **συνεχής** (continuous).
*   *Genitive:* Το αεροδρόμιο του **διεθνούς** δικτύου.

**The -ων / -ουσα / -ον Participles:**  
These are ancient active participles that act like adjectives (similar to the English "-ing"). 
*   **ενδιαφέρων** (Masc), **ενδιαφέρουσα** (Fem), **ενδιαφέρον** (Neut) = Interesting.
*   **υπάρχων** (existing), **μέλλων** (future).
