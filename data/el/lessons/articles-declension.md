# Articles Declension
Just like nouns, the Greek definite article ("the") must be declined to match the case of the noun it describes. This is one of the most important patterns to memorize, as it often provides the clearest clue to a noun's case.

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Singular (Ενικός)** |
| Nom | ο | η | το |
| Gen | του | της | του |
| Acc | το(ν) | τη(ν) | το |
| **Plural (Πληθυντικός)** |
| Nom | οι | οι | τα |
| Gen | των | των | των |
| Acc | τους | τις | τα |

::: info-box mt-12 mb-12

### Patterns to Watch

- **Shared Genitive:** Masculine and Neuter share the same Genitive Singular (του).
- **Universal Genitive Plural:** The Genitive Plural is **always** των for every single gender and noun in the Greek language!
- **Shared Plural Nom:** Masculine and Feminine share the same Nominative Plural (οι).

::: end
