# Articles Declension (Η Κλίση των Άρθρων)

You've made it to the final lesson of A1 Unit 2! Over the last few lessons, you have learned how masculine, feminine, and neuter nouns change shape between being the Subject (Nominative) and the Object (Accusative).

To wrap up this unit, we are going to look at the **Definite Articles** ("The") all in one place. 

If you memorize this table, you will be able to guess the gender and case of almost any noun you see, even if you don't know what the word means!

---

## 1. Singular Articles (Ενικός αριθμός)

| Case/Role | Masculine (He) | Feminine (She) | Neuter (It) |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Nom** (Subject) | **ο** | **η** | **το** |
| **Acc** (Object) | **το(ν)** | **τη(ν)** | **το** |
| Gen (Owner) | του | της | του |

**Notice the patterns:**
1. **The Neuter is lazy:** The neuter article **το** never changes between being the subject or the object.
2. **The "T" Rule for Objects:** In the Accusative case (when receiving the action), every single article starts with a **Τ** (το**ν**, **τ**ην, **τ**ο). If you see an article starting with T, it's probably the object!

---

## 2. Plural Articles (Πληθυντικός αριθμός)

Now let's look at what happens when there is more than one item.

| Case/Role | Masculine (They-m) | Feminine (They-f) | Neuter (They-n) |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Nom** (Subject) | **οι** | **οι** | **τα** |
| **Acc** (Object) | **τους** | **τις** | **τα** |
| Gen (Owner) | των | των | των |

**Notice the patterns:**
1. **The Shared "οι":** If you are talking about multiple men or multiple women who are doing an action (Subjects/Nominative), the article is exactly the same: **οι**. (e.g., *Οι άντρες* and *Οι γυναίκες*).
2. **The Shared "των":** If you are talking about the owners of something (Genitive), the article is **των** for *literally everything* in the Greek language. It never changes.
3. **The Neuter is still lazy:** The plural neuter article **τα** never changes between being the subject or the object.

## 🎯 A1 Unit 2 Summary

Congratulations! You now understand the core engine of the Greek language: **Declensions**. 

If you can remember the following three laws, you are ready to start conjugating verbs in Unit 3:
- [ ] Law 1: Nouns change their endings based on whether they are the "Doer" (Nominative) or the "Receiver" (Accusative).
- [ ] Law 2: You must always match the article (**ο, η, το**, etc.) to the noun's gender and case.
- [ ] Law 3: Almost all Masculine words drop their final **-ς** when they become the Object (Accusative). Feminine and Neuter words do not change.
