# Unit 1: The Greek Writing System Masterclass

Welcome to your first step toward mastering Greek! As an English speaker, learning the Greek alphabet is much easier than you might think. Over 25% of English vocabulary has Greek roots—you already know more Greek than you realize.

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to read and write Modern Greek: Single Letters, Digraphs (Sound Combinations), Accents, and Punctuation.

---

## 1. The 24 Letters
The Greek alphabet consists of 24 letters. To make them easier to learn, we can group them into three categories:

### Team A: The "Freebies"
These look and sound almost exactly like their English counterparts.

| Letter | Name | Sound | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Α α** | Alpha | [a] | **a**pple |
| **Ε ε** | Epsilon | [e] | p**e**t |
| **Ι ι** | Iota | [ee] | m**ee**t |
| **Κ κ** | Kappa | [k] | **k**ite |
| **Μ μ** | Mu | [m] | **m**om |
| **Ν ν** | Nu | [n] | **n**o |
| **Ο ο** | Omicron | [o] | t**o**p |
| **Τ τ** | Tau | [t] | **t**op |
| **Ζ ζ** | Zeta | [z] | **z**oo |

> [!TIP]
> **Pro-Tip for English Speakers:** The voiceless consonants **π [p], τ [t], and κ [k] are pronounced without "aspiration"**. Unlike in English, where you release a puff of air when saying words like pot or cat, in Greek these letters have no expulsion of breath, sounding more like the crisp 'p' in spot or 't' in stay.

### Team B: The "False Friends"
These look like English letters but represent different sounds.

| Letter | Name | Sound | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Β β** | Beta | [v] | **v**an (not 'B') |
| **Η η** | Eta | [ee] | m**ee**t (not 'H') |
| **Ρ ρ** | Rho | [r] | **r**olled R (not 'P') |
| **Χ χ** | Chi | [h] | Scottish **ch** (loch) |
| **Υ υ** | Upsilon | [ee] | m**ee**t (not 'Y' or 'U') |

### Team C: The "Aliens"
These shapes are unique to the Greek alphabet.

| Letter | Name | Sound | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Γ γ** | Gamma | [j] or [g] | **y**es (before e/i) |
| **Δ δ** | Delta | [th] | **th**is (soft) |
| **Θ θ** | Theta | [th] | **th**ink (hard) |
| **Λ λ** | Lambda | [l] | **l**ion |
| **Ξ ξ** | Xi | [ks] | bo**x** |
| **Π π** | Pi | [p] | **p**en |
| **Σ σ, ς** | Sigma | [s] or [z] | **s**un (z before β, γ, δ) |
| **Φ φ** | Phi | [f] | **f**ish |
| **Ψ ψ** | Psi | [ps] | o**ps** |
| **Ω ω** | Omega | [o] | t**o**p |

## 2. Digraphs (Letter Combinations)
Sometimes Greek glues two letters together to create a single new sound.

### Vowel Twins
- **αι** = [e] (αιτία - cause)
- **ει, οι, υι** = [ee] (είμαι - I am)
- **ου** = [oo] (ουρανός - sky)

> [!TIP]
> **The Tricky Vowel Twins (αυ & ευ):**  
> When **α** or **ε** are combined with **υ**, the υ transforms into a consonant sound! 
> - **αυ / ευ** sound like **[af] / [ef]** before voiceless consonants. 
> - **αυ / ευ** sound like **[av] / [ev]** before vowels or voiced consonants.

### Consonant Twins (The Hard Sounds)
Over centuries, some original sounds softened. Modern Greek uses these combinations to regain those "hard" sounds.
- **μπ** = B (μπαμπάς - dad)
- **ντ** = D (ντύνω - to dress)
- **γκ / γγ** = G (άγκυρα - anchor)
- **τσ** = TS (τσέπη - pocket)
- **τζ** = J / DZ (τζάκι - fireplace)

## 3. Accents & Punctuation
Greek is highly phonetic, and its punctuation marks are unique.

### The Tonos (´)
Every word with 2+ syllables has an accent mark to show the stressed syllable.
- **νερό** (ne-RÓ) - water
- **πρόβλημα** (PRÓ-vli-ma) - problem

### Punctuation Marks
- **The Question Mark (;):** The Greek question mark is the English semicolon.
- **The Upper Dot (·):** Used like a colon or semicolon for a medium break.
- **Quotation Marks (« »):** Used for dialogue and quotes.
