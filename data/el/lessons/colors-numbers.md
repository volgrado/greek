# Colors and Numbers (Χρώματα και Αριθμοί)

Adjectives are like chameleons: they change their endings to match the noun they are describing. 

What better way to practice this than with Colors and Numbers?

## 1. Colors (Τα Χρώματα)

Most colors in Greek act exactly like the adjectives you just learned. They follow the **-ος, -η, -ο** or **-ος, -α, -ο** patterns.

| English | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Red** | κόκκιν**ος** | κόκκιν**η** | κόκκιν**ο** |
| **Black** | μαύρ**ος** | μαύρ**η** | μαύρ**ο** |
| **Green** | πράσιν**ος** | πράσιν**η** | πράσιν**ο** |
| **White** | άσπρ**ος** | άσπρ**η** | άσπρ**ο** |
| **Yellow** | κίτριν**ος** | κίτριν**η** | κίτριν**ο** |
| **Grey** | γκρίζ**ος** | γκρίζ**α** | γκρίζ**ο** |

### The "Undeclinable" Colors
However, some colors were borrowed from foreign languages (like French or Italian). Remember what we learned about foreign nouns like *το ίντερνετ*? They are stubborn and refuse to change their endings. 

These colors **never change**. They look exactly the same whether they describe a man or a woman:
- **Μπλε** *(ble)* = Blue
- **Ροζ** *(roz)* = Pink
- **Καφέ** *(ka-fé)* = Brown

::: example-box 
**Ο μπλε** ουρανός. (The blue sky - Masculine)
**Η μπλε** θάλασσα. (The blue sea - Feminine)
**Το μπλε** αυτοκίνητο. (The blue car - Neuter)
::: end

## 2. Numbers 1 to 10 (Αριθμοί 1-10)

Like English, numbers in Greek are incredibly important. Here are the first ten numbers.

1. **Ένα** *(é-na)*
2. **Δύο** *(dí-o)*
3. **Τρία** *(trí-a)*
4. **Τέσσερα** *(tés-se-ra)*
5. **Πέντε** *(pén-te)*
6. **Έξι** *(é-xi)*
7. **Επτά** *(ep-tá)*
8. **Οκτώ** *(oc-tó)*
9. **Εννέα** *(en-né-a)*
10. **Δέκα** *(dé-ka)*

*Note: You will often hear Greeks say "εφτά", "οχτώ", and "εννιά" instead of the formal spellings above. Both are totally acceptable!*

### The Gender Test

Numbers are generally rigid. The number 5 is always *πέντε*, no matter what you are counting. 

But there are exceptions. Remember how the number '1' was the same as the indefinite article ('A')? 

Because the number 1 acts like an article, **it has to change its gender to match the noun it is counting!** Do you remember the table from Unit 2?

- **Ένας** άντρας (One man)
- **Μία** γυναίκα (One woman)
- **Ένα** παιδί (One child)

This rule also applies to the numbers 3 (*Τρεις / Τρία*) and 4 (*Τέσσερις / Τέσσερα*), but you don't need to memorize those forms until level A2. For now, just remember that the number 1 is a chameleon!

## 3. Practice Sentences

Let's put everything we've learned together! See if you can spot the Accusative Case (Direct Objects) and the Adjective Agreements working together.

::: example-box 
**Ο Γιώργος έχει ένα κόκκινο αυτοκίνητο.**
(George has a red car.)
Notice that *το* became *ένα*, and *κόκκινο* ends in *-ο* to match *αυτοκίνητο*.
::: end

::: example-box 
**Η Μαρία βλέπει τον μαύρο σκύλο.**
(Maria sees the black dog.)
Notice that *ο σκύλος* became *τον σκύλο* because of the Accusative rule, and *μαύρος* had to follow along and drop its -s!
::: end
