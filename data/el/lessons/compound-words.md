# Unit 30: Compound Words & Prefixation

Greek vocabulary is massive, but it's incredibly logical. Throughout its long history, Greek has always had a great facility for creating new words. Instead of inventing brand new words for every concept, Greeks build words like Lego bricks by modifying stems with prefixes, or snapping two smaller words together. 

Once you understand how the bricks connect, you can usually guess the meaning of a C1/C2 word even if you've never seen it before!

---

## 1. The Greek "Glue": The letter Omikron (o)
When you attach two Greek words together to form a compound, you almost always need "glue" to connect them. The elements that make up a compound are typically linked by the vowel **-ο-**. 

If the first word ends in a normal vowel (like -ος, -η, or -ι), that vowel simply turns into an **ο**. Then the second word attaches to it!

*   **βιβλίο** (book) + **πωλώ** (I sell) ➔ **βιβλιοπωλείο** (bookstore).
*   **μαχαίρι** (knife) + **πιρούνι** (fork) ➔ **μαχαιροπίρουνο** (knife and fork).
*   **Σάββατο** (Saturday) + **Κυριακή** (Sunday) ➔ **Σαββατοκύριακο** (weekend).

---

## 2. Prefixes (The Power Bricks)
There are dozens of highly active prefixes (mini-words) that you can slap onto the front of any word to instantly change its meaning.

### Α- / Αν- (The Negative / Un-)
This is the famous Alpha Privative. Greek uses a simple **α-** (if the next letter is a consonant) or **αν-** (if the next letter is a vowel). 

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **C1 Pronunciation Secret:** When adding the alpha privative to form a negative adjective, **the stress normally shifts to the third syllable from the end**!
> *   γνωστός (known) ➔ **άγνωστος** (unknown).
> *   φυσικός (natural) ➔ **αφύσικος** (unnatural).
> *   πιθανός (probable) ➔ **απίθανος** (improbable / amazing).

### Ξε- (The Reverser / Un-)
Prefixed to verbs to reverse the action (like tying and un-tying).
*   **κλειδώνω** (I lock) ➔ **ξεκλειδώνω** (I unlock).
*   **ντύνω** (I dress) ➔ **ξεντύνω** (I undress).
*   **διψώ** (I am thirsty) ➔ **ξεδιψώ** (I quench my thirst).

### Ξανα- (Again / Re-)
If you want to say "do it again" or "re-do", just add **ξανα-** to the verb!
*   **κάνω** (I do) ➔ **ξανακάνω** (I re-do).
*   **διαβάζω** (I read) ➔ **ξαναδιαβάζω** (I reread).

> [!TIP]
> **Bonus C1 Prefix: Κατα- (Very much)**  
> You can stick **κατα-** in front of adjectives to mean "extremely", or verbs to intensify the action. 
> *   άσπρος (white) ➔ **κάτασπρος** (very white).
*   βρέχω (I wet) ➔ **καταβρέχω** (I soak / drench).

---

## 3. Advanced Combinations
You can stick almost any parts of speech together to create powerful descriptive words. Always remember to use the glue **ο**!

*   **Adjective + Adjective:** **ασπρόμαυρος** (black and white).
*   **Adjective + Noun:** **κοκκινομάλλης** (red-haired), **στενόμυαλος** (narrow-minded).
*   **Adjective/Adverb + Participle:** **φρεσκοβαμμένος** (freshly painted), **μαυροντυμένος** (dressed in black).

### 🎭 The Action Combos (Verb + Verb)
Greek frequently creates compounds by sticking two verbs together, usually connected with the **ο** glue. 
*   **ανοίγω** (open) + **κλείνω** (close) ➔ **ανοιγοκλείνω** (I open and close).
*   **πηγαίνω** (go) + **έρχομαι** (come) ➔ **πηγαινοέρχομαι** (I come and go / I pacing).
*   **ανεβαίνω** (go up) + **κατεβαίνω** (go down) ➔ **ανεβοκατεβαίνω** (I go up and down).

---

## 4. Famous Greek Roots
Once you hit C1, you will see these roots everywhere. 

*   **-φίλος** (Lover / Friend): **ζωόφιλος** (animal lover), **φιλόξενος** (hospitable).
*   **-φόβος** (Fearer / Hater): **ξενοφοβία** (fear of foreigners), **υψοφοβία** (fear of heights).
*   **-λόγος** (Speaker / Student of): **ψυχολόγος** (psychologist).
*   **-γράφος** (Writer): **μυθιστοριογράφος** (novelist).
