# Compound Words (Σύνθετες Λέξεις)

Greek vocabulary is massive, but it's incredibly logical. Instead of inventing brand new words for every concept, Greeks build words like Lego bricks by snapping smaller words together. 

Once you understand how the bricks connect, you can usually guess the meaning of a C1/C2 word even if you've never seen it before!

These Lego-block words are called **Compound Words**.

---

## 1. The Greek "Glue": The letter Omikron (ο)

When you attach two Greek words together, you almost always need "glue" to connect them.

In Greek, the glue is the letter **ο** (omikron). 
If the first word ends in a normal vowel (like -ος or -η), that vowel simply turns into an **ο**. Then the second word attaches to it!

### Example 1: The Bookstore
- βιβλίο (book) + πωλώ (I sell)
- βιβλίο ➔ βιβλι- + glue **ο** ➔ βιβλι**ο**-
- βιβλι**ο**- + -πωλείο (selling place) = **βιβλιοπωλείο** (bookstore).

### Example 2: The Fish Tavern
- ψάρι (fish) + ταβέρνα (tavern)
- ψάρι ➔ ψαρ- + glue **ο** ➔ ψαρ**ο**-
- ψαρ**ο**- + ταβέρνα = **ψαροταβέρνα** (fish tavern).

## 2. Prefixes (The Power Bricks)

There are dozens of highly active prefixes (mini-words) that you can slap onto the front of any word to instantly completely change its meaning.

### Ξανα- (Again / Re-)
If you want to say "do it again" or "re-do", just add **ξανα-** to the verb!
- κάνω (I do) ➔ **ξανα**κάνω (I re-do)
- βλέπω (I see) ➔ **ξανα**βλέπω (I re-watch / see again)
- λέω (I say) ➔ **ξανα**λέω (I repeat)

### Α- / Αν- (The Negative / Un-)
This is the famous Alpha Privative. In English, we use "un-" or "in-" (unhappy, invisible). Greek uses a simple **α-** (if the next letter is a consonant) or **αν-** (if the next letter is a vowel).
- γνωστός (known) ➔ **ά**γνωστος (unknown)
- δίκαιος (fair/just) ➔ **ά**δικος (unfair)
- ικανός (capable) ➔ **αν**ίκανος (incapable)
- ηθικός (ethical) ➔ **αν**ήθικος (unethical)

### Ξε- (The Reverser / Un-)
This is the action reverser. While the Alpha Privative makes *adjectives* negative, **ξε-** is used to reverse the action of a *verb* (like tying and un-tying).
- κλειδώνω (I lock) ➔ **ξε**κλειδώνω (I unlock)
- δένω (I tie) ➔ **ξε**δένω (I untie)
- βάφω (I paint) ➔ **ξε**βάφω (I un-paint / strip paint)

## 3. Creating Adjectives from Nouns

You can stick two nouns together to create a powerful descriptive adjective. Always remember to use the glue `ο`!

- χρώμα (color) + ασήμι (silver) = **ασημόχρωμος** (silver-colored).
- καρδιά (heart) + πέτρα (stone) = **πετρόκαρδος** (stone-hearted).
- κεφάλι (head) + ελαφρύ (light) = **ελαφροκέφαλος** (light-headed).

## 4. Famous Greek Roots Every Learner Must Know

Once you hit C1, you will see these roots everywhere:

1. **-φίλος** (Lover / Friend):  
   - ζώο (animal) + φίλος = **ζωόφιλος** (animal lover).
   - ξένος (foreigner) + φίλος = **φιλόξενος** (hospitable).

2. **-φόβος** (Fearer / Hater):
   - ξένος + φόβος = **ξενοφοβία** (fear of foreigners).
   - ύψος (height) + φόβος = **υψοφοβία** (fear of heights).

3. **-λόγος** (Speaker / Studier of):
   - ψυχή (soul) + λόγος = **ψυχολόγος** (psychologist).
