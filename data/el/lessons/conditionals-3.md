# Conditional Sentences (Type 3 - The Past Unreal)

In Level B2, you learned about the Type 2 Conditional (The Unreal Dream). 
- **Type 2:** *If I had money (right now), I would buy a car (right now).*
- **Greek:** Αν είχα λεφτά, θα αγόραζα ένα αυτοκίνητο.

But what if you are talking about regret? What if it's too late because everything already happened in the past?

- **Type 3:** *If I had known (yesterday), I would have gone (yesterday).*

This is the **Past Unreal Conditional**. It is used to talk about things that never actually happened in the past, and what the alternative outcome would have been.

---

## 1. The English Headache vs The Greek Logic

In English, making a Type 3 conditional is a grammatical nightmare. You need a mix of Past Perfect and Future Perfect in the Past: "If I **had seen** you, I **would have said** hello."

In Greek, Type 3 is incredibly logical and easy to build. 

Do you remember the **Past Perfect tense** from Unit 23? That's when you take the past continuous helper verb `είχα` (I had) + the frozen simple stem `[verb]-ει`.
- **είχα δει** (I had seen)
- **είχα πάει** (I had gone)

To make a Type 3 conditional in Greek, you just use the **Past Perfect tense** for both sides of the sentence! 
To show that the second half is a hypothetical "would have", you just put **θα** in front of it!

### The Formula
**If [Past Perfect], then [Θα + Past Perfect].**

Let's look at the breakdown:

::: example-box 
**English:** If I had seen you, I would have spoken to you.

**Greek Step 1 (If):** Αν **είχα δει** εσένα...
**Greek Step 2 (Then):** ...**θα είχα μιλήσει** σε εσένα.

**Full Sentence:** Αν σε είχα δει, θα σου είχα μιλήσει.
::: end

::: example-box 
**English:** If he had read the book, he would have passed the test.
**Greek:** Αν **είχε διαβάσει** το βιβλίο, **θα είχε περάσει** τις εξετάσεις.
::: end

---

## 2. The Spoken Shortcut (The "Lazy" C1 Mix)

While the formula above (`Είχα X ➔ Θα είχα Y`) is grammatically perfect and highly recommended for C1 level writing, native speakers are often lazy when they speak.

Sometimes, in spoken Greek, people will mix Type 2 (Imperfect) and Type 3 (Past Perfect) together to talk about the past. 

For example, to say "If I had known, I wouldn't have come":

- **Strict Type 3:** Αν **είχα ξέρει**, δεν **θα είχα έρθει**. (Correct, but very long).
- **Spoken Greek:** Αν το **ήξερα**, δεν **θα ερχόμουν**. 

Wait, isn't "Αν το ήξερα" a Type 2 conditional (If I knew [right now])? 
Yes! But Greeks often use the Imperfect tense to refer to the Past Unreality because it's faster to say than the full Past Perfect. 

Context is everything. If the conversation is about a party that happened yesterday, "Αν το ήξερα" definitely means "If I had known (yesterday)".

### Recommended Usage for C1 Learners:
Stick to the strict Type 3 grammar (`είχα + ει ➔ θα είχα + ει`). It is always elegant, always correct, and it proves you have mastered advanced Greek syntax!
