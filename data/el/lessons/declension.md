# The Declension
In English, words like "friend" usually stay the same whether they are the subject or the object of a sentence. In Greek, nouns are **inflected**. This means they change their ending based on their grammatical role. This process is called **Declension**.

::: info-box mt-8

## The "Flexing" Language

Think of Greek as a "flexing" language. Just as you flex a muscle, words "flex" their endings to signal their function. If a word is the "doer" (subject), it has one ending; if it's the "receiver" (object), it has another.

::: end

## What gets declined?

In most Greek sentences, the following elements must agree and decline together:

::: content-list
**Articles:** ο, η, το...

**Nouns:** πατέρας, μητέρα...

**Adjectives:** καλός, μεγάλη...

**Pronouns:** αυτός, εγώ...

::: end

::: info-box mt-12 mb-12

### Standard vs. Irregular

Most nouns follow predictable patterns (Regular Nouns). However, some nouns are "indeclinable" (don't change) or follow their own unique rules. We will focus on the regular patterns first.

::: end
