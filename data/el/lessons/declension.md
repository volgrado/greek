# The Declension (Η Κλίση)

You now know that Greek nouns are tagged with **Cases** depending on what job they do in a sentence (Subject vs. Object).

But how do we actually "tag" them?

We change the ends of the words. This changing of word endings is a process called **Declension** (Η Κλίση).

When a Greek noun changes its role in a sentence, **three** things actually shape-shift together to match the new role:
1. The **Article** (the word for "the" or "a")
2. The **Noun** itself
3. Any **Adjectives** attached to the noun

---

## The "Flexing" Language

In English, nouns are incredibly stiff. The word "friend" looks the same whether they are doing something or having something done to them.

> **The friend** is here.
> I see **the friend**.

In Greek, nouns are flexible. They "flex" their endings to show you their grammatical muscle. 

Look at what happens to the masculine word **ο φίλος** (the friend).

### Scenario A: The Subject (Nominative)
The friend is doing the action.
> **Ο φίλος** είναι εδώ. (The friend is here.)
> Notice the **-ος** ending. This is his resting state.

### Scenario B: The Object (Accusative)
The friend is receiving the action.
> Βλέπω **τον φίλο**. (I see the friend.)
> Notice what happened! 
> 1. The article **ο** grew an 'n' and became **τον**. 
> 2. The noun **φίλος** dropped its 's' and became **φίλο**.

---

## Why does Greek do this?

It might seem like a lot of extra work just to say "I see the friend." But this flexibility gives Greek a superpower: **Word Order Freedom**.

In English, if you scramble the words "The dog bit the man", the sentence completely breaks down or changes meaning ("The man bit the dog"). 

In Greek, because the actor (**Ο σκύλος**) and the victim (**Τον άντρα**) are clearly tagged with their case endings, you can arrange the sentence however you like!

- **Ο σκύλος** δάγκωσε **τον άντρα**. (Normal)
- **Τον άντρα** δάγκωσε **ο σκύλος**. (Poetic/Emphatic)
- Δάγκωσε **ο σκύλος** **τον άντρα**. (Dramatic)

All three sentences mean exactly the same thing: The dog bit the man.

You don't have to guess how words change. Greek nouns belong to very predictable families based on their gender and ending.
