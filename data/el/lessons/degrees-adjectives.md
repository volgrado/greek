# Degrees of Comparison (Παραθετικά Επιθέτων)

In Level A1, you learned how to use basic adjectives to describe things: "The tall man" (Ο ψηλός άντρας).

But what if you want to say the man is **taller** than his friend? What if he is the **tallest** man in the world? Or what if he is just **very tall**?

These are called the "Degrees of Comparison". There are three levels:
1. **Positive Degree**: Tall.
2. **Comparative Degree**: Taller.
3. **Superlative Degree**: The tallest / Very tall.

---

## 1. The Comparative Degree (More / -er)

In English, we say "more beautiful" (for long words) or "tall**er**" (for short words).
Greek does exactly the same thing! There are two ways to form the comparative.

### Method A: The Easy Way (Using "πιο")
The easiest and most common way to say "more" in Greek is to just put the tiny word **πιο** (pi-o) in front of the adjective.

- ωραίος (beautiful) ➔ **πιο** ωραίος (more beautiful)
- έξυπνος (smart) ➔ **πιο** έξυπνος (smarter)
- ψηλός (tall) ➔ **πιο** ψηλός (taller)

*(Note: **πιο** never changes. The adjective still has to match the noun like a chameleon! "Η γυναίκα είναι **πιο ωραία**".)*

### Method B: The Suffix (-ότερος)
You can also add a suffix to the end of the word, just like the English "-er". 
Take the adjective, drop the 'ος', and add **-ότερος, -ότερη, -ότερο**.

- ωραίος ➔ ωραι**ότερος** (more beautiful)
- ψηλός ➔ ψηλ**ότερος** (taller)

*(Both methods are 100% correct, but Method A is much more common in spoken Greek).*

---

## 2. Using "Than" (από)

How do you use the Comparative in a sentence? You need the word "than".

In Greek, the word for "than" is **από** (from/than). The noun that follows `από` will always be in the Accusative Case (the Object case).

::: example-box 
**Ο Γιώργος είναι πιο ψηλός από τον Κώστα.**
(George is taller than Kostas.)
*(Literally: George is more tall from Kostas).*
::: end

::: example-box 
**Το σπίτι μου είναι μεγαλύτερο από το δικό σου.**
(My house is bigger than yours.)
::: end

---

## 3. The Superlative Degree (The Most / Very)

The Superlative degree has two different flavors in Greek.

### Flavor 1: Relative Superlative (The tallest)
If you want to say "the tallest in the group", you use the Comparative form, but you put the **Definite Article** (o, η, το) directly in front of it!

- πιο ψηλός (taller) ➔ **ο** πιο ψηλός (the tallest)
- ψηλότερος (taller) ➔ **ο** ψηλότερος (the tallest)

::: example-box 
**Ο Γιώργος είναι ο πιο ψηλός στην τάξη.**
(George is the tallest in the class.)
::: end

### Flavor 2: Absolute Superlative (Very tall)
If you just want to say someone is "very, very tall" (without comparing them to anyone specific), you can add the extreme suffix **-ότατος, -ότατη, -ότατο**.

- ψηλός (tall) ➔ ψηλ**ότατος** (extremely tall)
- ωραίος (beautiful) ➔ ωραι**ότατη** (exquisitely beautiful)

*(Pro-Tip: You can also just say **πολύ** [very] + normal adjective: πολύ ψηλός).*

---

## 4. The 3 Crazy Irregulars

There are three adjectives that are used so often, their comparative forms are almost completely irregular. You must memorize these:

1. **καλός** (good) ➔ **καλύτερος** (better) ➔ **ο καλύτερος** (best)
2. **κακός** (bad) ➔ **χειρότερος** (worse) ➔ **ο χειρότερος** (worst)
3. **μεγάλος** (big/old) ➔ **μεγαλύτερος** (bigger/older) ➔ **ο μεγαλύτερος** (biggest/oldest)
