# Degrees of Adjectives
In Greek, adjectives change their form to indicate intensity. There are three degrees: **Positive** (tall), **Comparative** (taller), and **Superlative** (tallest).

## 1. The Positive Degree (Θετικός)

This is the base form of the adjective as learned in the previous lesson.

- ωραίος (beautiful)
- καλός (good)

## 2. The Comparative Degree (Συγκριτικός)

There are two ways to form the comparative in Greek:

::: content-grid mt-8

### Method A: Using "πιο"

Simply place the word **πιο** (more) before the adjective. This works for almost all adjectives.

::: example-box
πιο ωραίος more beautiful / handsomer
::: end

### Method B: Using Suffixes

Add the suffix **-ότερος** to the root of the adjective.

::: example-box
ωραι **ότερος** more beautiful
::: end

::: end

## 3. The Superlative Degree (Υπεθετικός)

| Type | Formation | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Relative** | Article + "πιο" + Adj | ο πιο ωραίος (the most beautiful) |
| **Absolute** | Add **-ότατος** | ωραι **ότατος** (very beautiful) |

::: info-box mt-12 mb-12

### Common Irregulars

Some adjectives have unique comparative forms:

- καλός → καλύτερος (better)
- μεγάλος → μεγαλύτερος (bigger)

::: end
