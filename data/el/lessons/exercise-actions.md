# Unit 4-5: Action & The Accusative

This exercise is divided into four fluency-building activities to move you from theory to automaticity.

## 1. The Constrained Picture Exercise

*(A rainy street in Thessaloniki, Greece. People holding umbrellas and hurrying)*
![A rainy street in Thessaloniki](/assets/images/thessaloniki_rainy_street.png)

**Power Lexis:**

| Greek | Pronunciation | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Η βροχή** | *(i vro-chí)* | The rain |
| **Η ομπρέλα** | *(i om-bré-la)* | The umbrella |
| **Κρατάω** | *(kra-táo)* | I hold |
| **Τρέχω** | *(tré-kho)* | I run |

**Grammar Tags (Goals):**
1. Use one **Type A action verb** (e.g. κρατάω, τρέχω).
2. Include a **direct object in the Accusative case**.

> **Pro Tip:** Remember that feminine nouns keep the "α" or "η" in the accusative, but the definite article changes from "η" to "την".

Listen to the native speaker describing the scene. Your goal is to speak *at the exact same time*, mimicking the rhythm and speed.

«Βρέχει πολύ σήμερα. Ο άνθρωπος κρατάει την ομπρέλα.»
(It is raining a lot today. The man is holding the umbrella.)
</details>

**Why it works:**

* **κρατάει την ομπρέλα**: "Κρατάει" is the 3rd person singular of the Type A action verb "κρατάω". "Την ομπρέλα" is the direct object receiving the action, correctly inflected in the accusative case (changing from "Η ομπρέλα" to "Την ομπρέλα").

## 3. The Mutation Drill (Speed-Shift)

Read the "Base" sentence aloud. Then, look at the "Trigger" and instantly change the sentence mentally before checking the Target. This builds agility without overthinking.

| Base Sentence | Trigger | Target Sentence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Διαβάζω το βιβλίο.** <br> *(I read the book.)* | Change subject to **Ο δάσκαλος** | **Ο δάσκαλος διαβάζει το βιβλίο.** |
| **Κρατάει την ομπρέλα.** <br> *(He/She holds the umbrella.)* | Change subject to **Εγώ** (I) | **Κρατάω την ομπρέλα.** |
| **Τρέχω γρήγορα.** <br> *(I run fast.)* | Change subject to **Η γυναίκα** (The woman) | **Η γυναίκα τρέχει γρήγορα.** |
| **Βλέπει τον καφέ.** <br> *(He/She sees the coffee.)* | Change subject to **Εμείς** (We) | **Βλέπουμε τον καφέ.** |

---

## 4. The Grammar-Saturated Story

Read this short story three times: first for accuracy, second for comprehension, and third *as fast as possible*. This forces your brain to "soak" in the patterns of the Accusative Case and Type A verbs.

> «Βλέπω την τσάντα. Η γυναίκα κρατάει την τσάντα. Βλέπω τον καφέ. Ο άντρας πίνει τον καφέ. Εγώ έχω το νερό. Πίνω το νερό και βλέπω τον ουρανό. Κάνω μία βόλτα.»
