# Unit 1-3: Foundations

This exercise is divided into four fluency-building activities to move you from theory to automaticity.

## 1. The Constrained Picture Exercise

*(An old Greek grandmother cooking in a rustic, sunlight-filled kitchen)*
![Old Greek grandmother cooking](/assets/images/greek_grandmother_kitchen.png)

**Power Lexis:**

| Greek | Pronunciation | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Η γιαγιά** | *(i ya-yá)* | The grandmother |
| **Το φαγητό** | *(to fa-yi-tó)* | The food |
| **Είναι** | *(í-ne)* | He/She/It is |
| **Έχω** | *(é-kho)* | I have |

**Grammar Tags (Goals):**
1. Describe the scene using the verb **"to be"** (είμαι).
2. Describe an action/possession using the verb **"to have"** (έχω).

> **Pro Tip:** Keep it simple! Focus on creating short, complete sentences that use the definite article correctly.

«Η γιαγιά είναι στην κουζίνα. Έχει ωραίο φαγητό.»
(The grandmother is in the kitchen. She has nice food.)
</details>

**Why it works:**

* **Η γιαγιά είναι...**: Correct use of the feminine definite article "η" with the subject noun and the 3rd person singular present tense form of "είμαι".
* **Έχει ωραίο φαγητό**: The verb "έχω" is conjugated to the 3rd person singular ("έχει" - she has). "Ωραίο φαγητό" (nice food) follows as the related object of possession.

## 3. The Mutation Drill (Speed-Shift)

Read the "Base" sentence aloud. Then, look at the "Trigger" and instantly change the sentence mentally before checking the Target. This builds agility without overthinking.

| Base Sentence | Trigger | Target Sentence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Εγώ είμαι στην κουζίνα.** <br> *(I am in the kitchen.)* | Change subject to **Η γιαγιά** | **Η γιαγιά είναι στην κουζίνα.** |
| **Έχω ωραίο φαγητό.** <br> *(I have nice food.)* | Change subject to **Ο πατέρας** | **Ο πατέρας έχει ωραίο φαγητό.** |
| **Ο φίλος είναι εδώ.** <br> *(The friend is here.)* | Change subject to **Εμείς** (We) | **Εμείς είμαστε εδώ.** |
| **Η μητέρα έχει ένα βιβλίο.** <br> *(The mother has a book.)* | Change subject to **Εσύ** (You) | **Εσύ έχεις ένα βιβλίο.** |

---

## 4. The Grammar-Saturated Story

Read this short story three times: first for accuracy, second for comprehension, and third *as fast as possible*. This forces your brain to "soak" in the patterns of *to be* and *to have*.

> «Το σπίτι είναι μεγάλο. Η γιαγιά είναι στην κουζίνα. Έχει ωραίο φαγητό. Το παιδί είναι εδώ. Έχει ένα βιβλίο. Το βιβλίο είναι καλό. Η μητέρα είναι και αυτή εδώ. Είναι όλοι καλά!»
