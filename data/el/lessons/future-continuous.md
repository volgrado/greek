# Unit 11: Future Tenses (Θα) & Aspect

In Unit 10, you learned the magic of **Aspect** (The Video vs. The Photo). Now, we are going to apply that exact same logic to the future. 

In English, we just say "I will play." Greek demands more precision: are you going to play regularly as a habit (a Video), or are you talking about one specific game tomorrow (a Photo)?

---

## 1. The Future Particle: Θα

To talk about the future in Greek, you don't learn a whole new set of verb endings. Instead, you just use the tiny magic particle **θα** (which means "will") and place it right in front of the verb!

- **Παίζω** (I play) ➔ **Θα παίζω** (I will be playing).

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **The Pronoun Sandwich**  
> **Θα** loves being right next to the verb. The ONLY things allowed to come between **θα** and the verb are short object pronouns (like "me", "it", "him"). When this happens, they form a little sandwich!
> 
> - *Θα* **το** *κάνω.* (I will do **it**).
> - *Θα* **σου το** *δώσω.* (I will give **it to you**).

---

## 2. The Continuous Future (The "Video")

Use this future tense when you want to talk about a habit, a routine, or an action that will be ongoing in the future. 

**The Formula:** **Θα** + Present Tense Verb (The Continuous, "Video" Stem)

- Θα **διαβάζω** κάθε μέρα. (I will *be reading* every day).
- Κάθε Τετάρτη θα **τρώμε** μπέργκερ. (Every Wednesday we will *eat* burgers).

---

## 3. The Simple Future (The "Photo")

Use this future tense for a single, specific event that will happen and be completed at a particular point in time. This is by far the most common way to talk about the future!

**The Formula:** **Θα** + The Simple ("Photo") Stem

- Θα **διαβάσω** το βιβλίο αύριο. (I will *read* the book tomorrow—just once).
- Θα **φάμε** μια πίτσα απόψε. (We will *eat* a pizza tonight).

> [!CAUTION]
> **The Augment Trap**  
> We will learn about the Past Tense in a few units, but here is a massive warning: Many students try to build the Simple Future by borrowing the Simple Past exact form. But the Past Tense often has an extra "ε-" at the front (called an augment). **You must NEVER use the past tense 'ε-' in the future!** It is strictly a time machine for the past.
> 
> - *Past:* **έ**γραψα (I wrote).
> - *Future:* θα γράψω (I will write — *The 'ε' is stripped away!*). 

---

## 4. How to Say "I won't" (Negation)

Remember the golden rule from Unit 10? Verbs after *να* take *μη(ν)*. 
But **θα** is different! To negate a future sentence, you use your old friend **δεν**, placing it right before *θα*.

- **Δεν θα παίζω ποδόσφαιρο πια.** (I won't be playing football anymore - *habit*).
- **Δεν θα παίξω αύριο.** (I won't play tomorrow - *single event*).
- **Δεν θα σου το δώσω.** (I won't give it to you).

---

## 5. Speak Like a Native: The Probability Trick

Greeks don't *just* use **θα** for the future. They frequently use it to express probability, guessing, or supposing something right now! It works exactly like the English "must be" or "must have".

- *Somebody knocks on the door:* **Θα είναι** ο Γιώργος. (It *must be* George).
- *They are late:* **Θα έφυγαν.** (They *must have left*).
