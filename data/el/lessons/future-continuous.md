# Unit 11: Future Tenses (Θα) & Aspect

In Unit 10, you learned the magic of Aspect. Now, we apply that "Video vs. Photo" logic to the future. In Greek, we don't just say "I will play"; we specify if it's an ongoing habit or a one-time completed thing.

---

## 1. The Future Particle: Θα
Just like *να* and *ας*, the word **θα** is a particle that sits right in front of the verb to express actions expected to take place in the future. It translates to the English "will".

*   **English:** I will play.
*   **Greek:** **Θα** [verb].

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **The Pronoun Sandwich**  
> The particle *θα* can only be separated from the verb by weak object pronouns. If you have weak pronouns (like "me", "it", "him"), they must sneak **between** *θα* and the verb.
*   *Θα το κάνω.* (I will do it).
*   *Θα σου το δώσει.* (He will give it to you).

---

## 2. Continuous (Imperfective) Future: The Video
If you want to talk about a habit, a routine, or something you will be doing repeatedly or continuously in the future, use the **Continuous (Imperfective) Future**.

**Formula:** Θα + Present Tense Verb
*   Θα **διαβάζω** κάθε μέρα. (I will [be reading] every day).
*   Κάθε Τετάρτη θα **τρώμε** στην ταβέρνα. (Every Wednesday we will eat at the taverna).

---

## 3. Simple (Perfective) Future: The Photo
If you are talking about a single, specific event that will happen and be completed at a particular point in time, use the **Simple (Perfective) Future**. 

**Formula:** Θα + Dependent Form (Perfective Non-Past Stem + Present Endings)
*   Θα **διαβάσω** το βιβλίο αύριο. (I will read the book tomorrow—once).
*   Θα **συναντήσω** τη Μαίρη αύριο. (I will meet Mary tomorrow).

> [!CAUTION]
> **The Augment Trap (No Past Markings!)**  
> Even though the Simple Future relies on the perfective stem (often found by looking at the Simple Past tense), you must **NEVER add the past tense augment** (the prefix **ε-**) to the future. The augment is strictly a past tense marker!
*   *Simple Past:* **έ**γραψα (I wrote).
*   *Simple Future:* θα γράψω (I will write - *Notice the 'ε' is gone!*). 

---

## 4. The Negation Combo
To negate a future sentence ("I will not" / "I won't"), you must use the indicative negative particle **δεν**. It is always placed immediately before **θα**.

*   **Δεν θα παίζω** (I won't be playing regularly).
*   **Δεν θα παίξω** (I won't play this specific time).
*   **Δεν θα σου το δώσει** (He won't give it to you).

> [!TIP]
> **Bonus Native Secret: "Θα" for Probability**  
> Greeks don't just use *θα* for the future. They also use it to express probability or supposition, similar to the English "must" or "must have"!
*   *Θα έφτασε.* (He must have arrived - *using the Simple Past*).
*   *Θα είναι σπίτι.* (He must be at home - *using the Present*).
