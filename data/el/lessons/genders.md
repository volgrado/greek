# The Genders (Τα Γένη) - Exceptions and Rules

In the last lesson, you learned that **Ο**, **Η**, and **Το** dictate whether a word is masculine, feminine, or neuter. You also learned that the ending of the word usually gives away the gender.

But like any language, Greek has a few exceptions. To achieve fluency, you need to know how to spot the "spies"—words that look like one gender but are actually another.

---

## 1. The "Feminine Spies" ending in -ος

The most common masculine ending is **-ος** (like *ο φίλος* = the friend). However, there is a small class of very common, very important words that end in **-ος** but are completely **Feminine (Η)**.

These are almost always geographical terms or words stolen from Ancient Greek.

::: content-grid

::: content-card
**η οδός**  
*(ee o-dós)*  
the street (formal name)
::: end

::: content-card
**η λεωφόρος**  
*(ee le-o-fó-ros)*  
the avenue
::: end

::: content-card
**η οθόνη**  
Wait, wait. It's **η μέθοδος** (the method), **η έξοδος** (the exit).
Let's stick to the common ones!
::: end

::: end

**The Three Most Common Feminine "-ος" Words:**
1. **η οδός** (the street - used for addresses)
2. **η λεωφόρος** (the avenue)
3. **η ψήφος** (the vote)
4. Certain islands: **η Κύπρος** (Cyprus), **η Ρόδος** (Rhodes)

> [!CAUTION]
> If a Greek asks you where you live, you would say "Στην **οδό** Ερμού" (On Ermou **street**). Even though it ends in *-ο*, it behaves according to feminine grammatical rules!

## 2. Foreign Loanwords

What happens when Greek adopts a word from English or French? For example, "computer", "internet", or "bar"?

Greek doesn't know what gender these words are supposed to be. So, it simply dumps almost all foreign loanwords into the **Neuter (Το)** category.

Furthermore, foreign loanwords **never change their shape**. They are invariable.

::: content-card
**το ίντερνετ** *(to in-ter-net)* = the internet  
**το μπαρ** *(to bar)* = the bar  
**το σινεμά** *(to si-ne-má)* = the cinema  
**το ταξί** *(to ta-xí)* = the taxi
::: end

Even though *το ίντερνετ* ends in a consonant (which native Greek neuter words never do), it is neuter purely because it's a foreign word.

---

## 3. The Neuter "-ος" Words

We just talked about feminine words ending in **-ος**. Well, there's another curveball. Some neuter words also end in **-ος**! 

These describe abstract concepts or states of being. 

**The Three Most Common Neuter "-ος" Words:**
1. **το λάθος** (the mistake)
2. **το βάθος** (the depth / background)
3. **το μέγεθος** (the size)
4. **το κράτος** (the state / nation)

These are very advanced for your current level, but you will hear Greeks say *«Κάνεις λάθος»* (You are making a mistake). Notice it receives the neuter article **το**, not the masculine *ο*.

---

## 🎯 Wrap-Up Checklist

Don't let these exceptions scare you. 95% of the time, the rules from the last lesson apply flawlessly. 

Just remember:
- [ ] If a word is borrowed from English (like *taxi* or *internet*), it is always **Το**.
- [ ] Words like **η οδός** (street) are feminine despite ending in *-ος*.
- [ ] Words like **το λάθος** (mistake) are neuter despite ending in *-ος*.
