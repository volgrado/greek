# The Genders
Every Greek noun belongs to one of three grammatical categories: Masculine, Feminine, or Neuter.

## 01. Masculine (Ο)

- Ends generally in -ος, -as, or -ης.
- Examples: ο δρόμος (street), ο πατέρας (father).

## 02. Feminine (Η)

- Ends generally in -η or -a.
- Examples: η μητέρα (mother), η καρδιά (heart).

## 03. Neuter (Το)

- Ends generally in -o, -ι, or -μα.
- Examples: το παιδί (child), το σπίτι (house).

**Note on Ambiguity** While most -ος nouns are masculine, some are feminine (η οδός). The article is your only fail-safe guide.
