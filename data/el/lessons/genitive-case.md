# Unit 15: The Genitive Case (Possession & Relationships)

You’ve mastered **Subjects** (Nominative) and **Objects** (Accusative). Now, we enter the world of **Ownership**. The Genitive case is the Greek equivalent of the English apostrophe "s" ('s) or the word "of."

---

## 1. The Genitive Articles
When a person or thing becomes an "Owner," the article must change. There is a very consistent pattern here:

| Gender | Singular Owner | Plural Owners |
| :--- | :---: | :---: |
| **Masculine** | **του** | **των** |
| **Feminine** | **της** | **των** |
| **Neuter** | **του** | **των** |

> [!TIP]
> **The Universal Plural**  
> In the Genitive plural, gender doesn't exist! Every single plural owner uses the article **των** and ends in **-ων**.  
> - Των φίλων, των δασκάλων, των παιδιών.

---

## 2. Noun Endings (Singular)
How does the noun itself change?

- **Masculine (-ος, -ας, -ης)**: Drops the final **-ς**. (e.g., ο πατέρας ➔ **του πατέρα**). *Nouns in -ος change to -ου.*
- **Feminine (-η, -α)**: **Always adds an -ς**. (e.g., η Μαρία ➔ **της Μαρίας**).
- **Neuter (-ο, -ι)**: Changes to **-ου** or adds a syllable (e.g., το παιδί ➔ **του παιδιού**).

---

## 3. Possession: The "Of" Rule
In English, we say "The teacher's book." In Greek, you must say "The book **of the** teacher." The object being owned comes first!

- **Ο σκύλος του Γιώργου** (George's dog / The dog of George).
- **Το σπίτι της Μαρίας** (Maria's house / The house of Maria).
- **Το χρώμα του αυτοκινήτου** (The car's color / The color of the car).

---

## 4. Indirect Objects (To Me / For Me)
As you learned in Unit 13, the Genitive also marks the **Receiver** of an action.
- **Μου** δίνεις... (Give **to me**...)
- **Του** λέω... (I am telling **him**...)
