# The Verb 'To Have' (Έχω)

Now that you know how to say "I am" (**είμαι**), it's time to learn the second most important verb in Greek: "I have" (**έχω**).

Together, these two verbs will unlock hundreds of sentences!

The dictionary form of this verb is **έχω** *(é-ho)*. 

---

## 1. Conjugating "Έχω" (I have)

Remember the "Pro-Drop" rule from the last lesson? You don't need to say "Εγώ" (I) or "Εσύ" (You) before the verb. The ending of the verb tells us exactly who is doing the having.

Look closely at how the end of the word changes:

| Pronoun | Verb | English | Pronunciation |
|---|---|---|---|
| (Εγώ) | **έχω** | I have | *(é-ho)* |
| (Εσύ) | **έχεις** | You have | *(é-his)* |
| (Αυτό/ή/ό) | **έχει** | He/She/It has | *(é-hi)* |
| (Εμείς) | **έχουμε** | We have | *(é-hou-me)* |
| (Εσείς) | **έχετε** | You have | *(é-he-te)* |
| (Αυτοί/ές/ά) | **έχουν(ε)** | They have | *(é-houn)* |

> [!TIP]
> **The Secret "O" Ending**
> Almost every verb in the Greek dictionary ends in **-ω** (the letter Omega). Just like **έχω**! 
> If you see a verb ending in **-ω**, you instantly know it means "I [do something]". 
> - παίζ**ω** (I play)
> - τρέχ**ω** (I run)
> - θέλ**ω** (I want)

---

## 2. Using "Έχω" in Sentences

Just like in English, we use "έχω" to talk about possession, family, and physical objects.

Here is where your knowledge from Unit 2 (The Accusative Case) kicks in! 

When you have something, that "something" is the Object of the sentence. It is receiving the action of being had. 

Therefore, **whatever you have must be in the Accusative Case**. 

Look at the examples below. Notice how the articles are **τον**, **την**, or **ένα**:

::: example-box 
**Έχω** ένα σκύλο. 
I have **a** dog.
::: end

::: example-box 
**Έχεις** το βιβλίο;
Do you have **the** book?
::: end

::: example-box 
Ο Γιώργος **έχει** μία αδερφή.
George has **a** sister.
::: end

::: example-box 
**Έχουμε** τον χρόνο.
We have **the** time.
::: end

---

## 3. The Power Combo: Έχω + Είμαι

With just these two verbs and the nouns you've learned so far, you can build full, natural conversations!

**Person A:**
*Είσαι* εδώ; (Are you here?)
**Person B:**
Ναι, *είμαι* εδώ. (Yes, I am here.)
**Person A:**
*Έχεις* τον χάρτη; (Do you have the map?)
**Person B:**
Όχι, ο Γιάννης *έχει* τον χάρτη. (No, Giannis has the map.)
