# Idiomatic Expressions & Proverbs

Congratulations. You have reached the final lesson of the C2 Greek Curriculum. 
You know the grammar, you know the vocabulary, and you can understand complex, advanced sentences.

But true fluency isn't just about grammar. It's about culture. 
Greek relies heavily on idioms, metaphors, and ancient proverbs that make absolutely no sense when translated literally.

Here are the most iconic Greek idioms you must know. 

---

## 1. Expressions of Dismissal & Frustration

When Greeks are annoyed, they don't hold back. 

### Τρέχα γύρευε (Run and seek)
**Meaning:** "It's a lost cause" or "Good luck with that." 
Used when a situation is hopelessly complicated or something is impossible to find.
- *Πού είναι ο διευθυντής;* (Where is the manager?) 
- *Τρέχα γύρευε...* (Good luck finding out...)

### Κάνω την πάπια (I play the duck)
**Meaning:** "I play dumb" or "I pretend not to know."
Used when someone is ignoring an issue or pretending they have no idea what is going on.
- Του είπα να πληρώσει και αυτός **κάνει την πάπια**! 
(I told him to pay and he is playing dumb!)

### Τον έγραψα στα παλιά μου τα παπούτσια 
*(I wrote him on my old shoes)*
**Meaning:** "I completely ignored him" / "I don't give a damn about him."
A very strong (but not offensive) way to say you completely disregarded someone's opinion or presence.

## 2. Expressions of Trouble / Disaster

### Τα έκανα θάλασσα (I made a sea out of it)
**Meaning:** "I messed everything up" / "I made a huge mistake."
Used when you completely botch a task or a test.
- Πώς πήγαν οι εξετάσεις; (How did the exams go?)
- **Τα έκανα θάλασσα...** (I messed it all up...)

### Έγινε της κακομοίρας (It became of the ill-fated woman)
**Meaning:** "All hell broke loose" / "It was absolute chaos."
Used to describe a crazy fight, a massive argument, or a chaotic situation.
- Όταν έμαθε την αλήθεια, **έγινε της κακομοίρας**.
(When he learned the truth, all hell broke loose).

## 3. Classic Greek Proverbs

Proverbs (Παροιμίες) are pieces of old wisdom passed down through generations. Greeks use them constantly in daily speech to prove a point.

### Όποιος βιάζεται σκοντάφτει.
*(Whoever hurries, trips).*
**Meaning:** Haste makes waste. Take your time and do things properly.

### Αλλού τα κακαρίσματα κι αλλού γεννούν οι κότες.
*(The clucking is over here, but the hens are laying eggs over there).*
**Meaning:** The real action is happening somewhere completely different from where the noise is. (Used when someone is distracted by a fake issue, or a politician is making a decoy statement).

### Έλειπε ο Μάρτης από την Σαρακοστή;
*(Could March be missing from Lent?)*
**Meaning:** Used when someone always, inevitably shows up to an event (usually uninvited or predictably). "There he is again... of course he's here."

---

## 4. The Ultimate Greek Word: Φιλότιμο

If there is one concept you take away from Greek culture, let it be **Φιλότιμο** (Filótimo). 

Literally, it means "Friend of honor". 
There is no direct translation in English. 

It describes a deep, unconditional sense of personal pride, duty, courage, respect, and doing the right thing *simply because it is the right thing to do*, even if it requires personal sacrifice. 

- Δεν το έκανε για τα λεφτά, το έκανε από **φιλότιμο**. 
(He didn't do it for the money, he did it out of *filotimo*).
