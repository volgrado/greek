# The Imperative (Η Προστακτική)

The Imperative Mood is used to give commands, orders, or strong advice. 

In English, we just yell the base verb at someone: "Run!", "Close the door!", "Stop!"

In Greek, giving a command is deeply tied to **Aspect** (Continuous vs. Simple). 
Are you commanding someone to do something *right now, once*? Or are you commanding someone to do something *repeatedly as a habit*?

Let's look at the Simple (One-time) Commands first, because they are the most common.

---

## 1. The Simple Command (Do it now!)

If you want someone to do a single action and finish it (like "Shut the door" or "Write your name"), you must build a command using the **Simple Stem** (The Aorist Stem).

There are two people you can give commands to: 
1. **Εσύ** (You singular - Informal)
2. **Εσείς** (You plural / You formal)

### Giving Informal Commands (Εσύ)
To tell one person to do something right now:
Take the Simple Stem, and add an **-ε** or an **-ε** sound.
- γράφω ➔ γράψ- ➔ **Γράψε!** (Write!)
- διαβάζω ➔ διαβάσ- ➔ **Διάβασε!** (Read!)
- κλείνω ➔ κλείσ- ➔ **Κλείσε!** (Close!)

### Giving Formal/Plural Commands (Εσείς)
To tell multiple people (or someone you respect) to do something right now:
Take the Simple Stem, and add **-τε** (or **-στε** for verbs that changed to 's').
- **Γράψτε!** (Write, guys!)
- **Διαβάστε!** (Read, class!)
- **Κλείστε!** (Close it, sir!)

---

## 2. The Continuous Command (Keep doing it!)

What if you want to say "Keep running!", or "Always read before bed"?

You are asking for a continuous, ongoing habit or action. Therefore, you must use the **Continuous Stem** (The Present Stem).

### Giving Informal Commands (Εσύ)
Take the Present Stem, and literally just use the "You" ending without the final -s!
- **Τρέχε!** (Keep running!)
- **Διάβαζε!** (Read [every day]!)

### Giving Formal/Plural Commands (Εσείς)
This is the easiest one of all. Exploring the present tense, the Formal Command is **identical** to the normal Present Tense "You guys" form!
- **Τρέχετε!** (Keep running, guys!)
- **Διαβάζετε!** (Keep reading, class!)

---

## 3. The Negative Command (Don't do it!)

Here is the best news you will hear all day. 

Greek does not have special negative command words. If you want to say "Don't do that!", you just use the **Subjunctive Mood** (Unit 17) with the negative word **μη(ν)**!

That's it! "Don't run" is literally just "Not you-run".

- **Μην κλείσεις** την πόρτα! (Don't close the door [this once]!)
- **Μην τρέχεις!** (Don't run [in general]!)

*(Remember: We use the word Μην instead of Δεν whenever we are giving commands or expressing wishes!)*

---

## 4. The 5 Crazy Irregulars

Because commands are used all the time in daily life, many of the most common verbs have totally irregular shapes when yelled as a command.

You must memorize these 5 everyday commands. They do not follow the rules!

1. **Λέω** (I say) ➔ **Πες!** (Say it!) / **Πείτε!** (Say it, guys!)
2. **Βλέπω** (I see) ➔ **Δες!** (Look!) / **Δείτε!** (Look, guys!)
3. **Βρίσκω** (I find) ➔ **Βρες!** (Find it!) / **Βρείτε!** (Find it, guys!)
4. **Πίνω** (I drink) ➔ **Πιες!** (Drink!) / **Πιείτε!** (Drink, guys!)
5. **Έρχομαι** (I come) ➔ **Έλα!** (Come here!) / **Ελάτε!** (Come here, guys!)

*(Pro-Tip: "Έλα!" is probably the most commonly used word in Greece. It means "Come here", "Come on", "Hello [on the phone]", and "Get out of here / No way!")*
