# Unit 16: The Imperative Mood (Giving Orders)

The Imperative Mood is used to give commands, orders, or strong advice. In Greek, just like in the Future and Past, giving a command is deeply tied to **Aspect** (Video vs. Photo).

---

## 1. Simple Commands (The Photo: Do it once!)
This is the most common way to give a command. You use the **Simple Stem** (Aorist) that you learned in Unit 12.

| Person | Ending | Example: **γράφω** ➔ **γράψ-** |
| :--- | :---: | :--- |
| **Εσύ** (Informal) | **-ε** | **Γράψε!** (Write!) |
| **Εσείς** (Formal/Plural) | **-τε** | **Γράψτε!** (Write!) |

*Examples:*
- **Κλείσε** την πόρτα! (Close the door!)
- **Διαβάστε** το βιβλίο! (Read the book!)

---

## 2. Continuous Commands (The Video: Keep doing it!)
Used for habits or ongoing actions. You use the **Continuous Stem** (Present).

- **Τρέχε!** (Keep running!)
- **Διάβαζε!** (Keep reading [as a habit]!)

> [!TIP]
> **Formal/Plural Continuous**: This form is identical to the normal Present tense!  
> - **Τρέχετε!** (Keep running, everyone!)

---

## 3. The 5 Absolute Irregulars
Like most languages, the most common commands in Greek are irregular. You MUST memorize these:

1. **Λέω** (Say) ➔ **Πες! / Πείτε!**
2. **Βλέπω** (Look) ➔ **Δες! / Δείτε!**
3. **Βρίσκω** (Find) ➔ **Βρες! / Βρείτε!**
4. **Πίνω** (Drink) ➔ **Πιες! / Πιείτε!**
5. **Έρχομαι** (Come) ➔ **Έλα! / Ελάτε!**

---

## 4. Negative Commands: Μην
To say "Don't," you use the magic particle **μην** (instead of *δεν*) followed by the Subjunctive form.

- **Μην κλείσεις** την πόρτα. (Don't close the door—this time).
- **Μην τρέχεις.** (Don't run—habitually).
