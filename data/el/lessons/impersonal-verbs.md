# Impersonal Verbs & Expressions (Απρόσωπα Ρήματα)

Every sentence needs a subject, right? "I eat", "The dog barks", "The man runs".

But what happens when there isn't a clear subject? 
- "It is raining." (What is raining? The sky?)
- "It is forbidden." (What is forbidden? The universe?)
- "It seems that..." (What seems?)

In Greek, when there is no specific person doing the action, we use a special group of verbs called **Impersonal Verbs**. 

Because "It" is the imaginary subject, these verbs only ever exist in the **3rd Person Singular** (the *Αυτό* form).

---

## 1. The Two Titans: Πρέπει and Μπορεί

You have already been using the two most common impersonal verbs since Level A1! 

- **Πρέπει (να)**: "It is necessary that..." ➔ **Must / Have to**
- **Μπορεί (να)**: "It is possible that..." ➔ **May / Might**

### The Subjunctive Trigger
Because impersonal verbs don't have a real subject, they almost always trigger the Subjunctive Mood (the word **να**). 

The verb that comes *after* **να** tells us who the real subject of the sentence is!

::: example-box 
**English:** I must go.
**Greek (Literal):** It is necessary that I go.
**Greek:** **Πρέπει να** πάω. 
::: end

::: example-box 
**English:** He might sleep.
**Greek (Literal):** It is possible that he sleeps.
**Greek:** **Μπορεί να** κοιμηθεί.
::: end

> [!WARNING]
> **Μπορώ vs Μπορεί**
> The verb "to be able to" exists as a normal, personal verb (*μπορώ* - I can). But when it is frozen in the 3rd person (*μπορεί*), it loses its personal owner and translates to "it is possible" / "might". 
> - **Μπορώ να** πάω. (I can go - I have the ability).
> - **Μπορεί να** πάω. (I might go - It is a possibility).

## 2. Other Common Impersonal Verbs

Here is a list of extremely common impersonal verbs that you will hear daily. 

Remember: they only ever exist in this frozen **-ει** form, and they are usually followed by **να**!

| Greek Verb | Literal Meaning | Real Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Απαγορεύεται** (να) | It is forbidden | **You are not allowed to...** |
| **Επιτρέπεται** (να) | It is permitted | **You are allowed to...** |
| **Φαίνεται** (ότι) | It seems | **It looks like...** |
| **Αξίζει** (να) | It is worth | **It's worth...** |
| **Συμβαίνει** (να) | It happens | **It happens to...** |
| **Πρόκειται** (για / να) | It is about | **It is about / It is going to...** |

::: example-box 
**Απαγορεύεται να καπνίζεις εδώ.**
(It is forbidden to smoke here / You are not allowed to smoke here).
::: end

::: example-box 
**Αξίζει να διαβάσεις αυτό το βιβλίο.**
(It is worth reading this book / This book is worth reading).
::: end

::: example-box 
**Πρόκειται να βρέξει.**
(It is going to rain).
::: end

---

## 3. Impersonal Expressions (Είναι + Adjective)

You don't always need a special verb to create an impersonal sentence. You can just use the frozen word **Είναι** (It is) followed by an adjective!

This also directly triggers the **να** subjunctive structure.

- **Είναι δύσκολο να...** (It is difficult to...)
- **Είναι εύκολο να...** (It is easy to...)
- **Είναι λογικό να...** (It makes sense to...)
- **Είναι κρίμα να...** (It's a pity to...)

::: example-box 
**Είναι δύσκολο να μάθεις ελληνικά, αλλά αξίζει!**
(It is difficult to learn Greek, but it's worth it!)
::: end
