# Unit 7: Asking Questions (Τι, Πού, Ποιος)

You now have a solid vocabulary and know how to conjugate verbs. The final step in your A1 foundation is learning how to ask for things. In Greek, this involves two parts: a special punctuation mark and "Question Words" (interrogative pronouns and adverbs).

---

## 1. The Greek Question Mark (;)
The first thing you must memorize is that the English semicolon (;) is the Greek question mark. The English question mark (?) does not exist in Greek. Be careful not to confuse the two when reading!

*   **Πώς είσαι;** (How are you?)
*   **Τι κάνεις;** (What are you doing?)

---

## 2. The Fixed "W" Words (Indeclinable)
These words are easy because they are indeclinable—they never change their shape regardless of gender, number, or case.

*   **Τι** (ti) = What? (e.g., *Τι είναι αυτό;*)
*   **Πού** (poo) = Where? (e.g., *Πού είσαι;*)
*   **Πότε** (pó-te) = When? (e.g., *Πότε φεύγουμε;*)
*   **Πώς** (pos) = How? (e.g., *Πώς είσαι;*)
*   **Γιατί** (ya-tí) = Why? (e.g., *Γιατί λες όχι;*)

> [!TIP]
> **Accent Mystery:** You learned in Unit 1 that 1-syllable words generally don't get accents. **Πού** and **Πώς** are rare exceptions! They keep the accent only to differentiate them from their identical twins **που** (the relative pronoun "that/who") and **πως** (the conjunction "that"). *(Notice that 'Τι' does not have a twin, so it never takes an accent).*

---

## 3. The Chameleon "W" Words (Declinable)
Just like adjectives, some question words are "chameleons". They must perfectly match the gender, number, and case of the noun you are asking about.

### "Who" or "Which" (Ποιος)
Pronounced as one syllable: *pyos*. It declines like an adjective with the endings -ος, -α, -ο. 
*   **Ποιος** είναι ο άντρας; (Who is the man?)
*   **Ποια** είναι η γυναίκα; (Who is the woman?)
*   **Ποιο** παιδί; (Which child?)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Accusative Alert!** If "Who" is the object receiving the action, it drops its -ς and adds a **-ν**, becoming **Ποιον**. 
> *   *Ποιον βλέπεις;* (Who(m) do you see?)

> [!NOTE]
> **How to ask "Whose?"**  
> To ask "Whose?", you must use the Genitive (possession) case. You can use the formal word **Τίνος** (for masculine and neuter), or the very common colloquial forms: **ποιανού** (whose - masc/neut) and **ποιανής** (whose - fem).
> *   *Ποιανού είναι αυτό το βιβλίο;* (Whose book is this?)

### "How Much" or "How Many" (Πόσος)
This word declines like an adjective with the endings -ος, -η, -ο. 
*   **Πόσο** κάνει; (How much does it cost? - *Neuter*)
*   **Πόση** ζάχαρη θέλεις; (How much sugar do you want? - *Feminine*)
*   **Πόσα** παιδιά έχεις; (How many children do you have? - *Neuter plural*)

---

## 4. The "Preposition First" Rule
In English, you can end a question with a preposition (e.g., "Where did you buy those shoes *from*?" or "Who are you talking *to*?"). 

**In Greek, you can NEVER separate the preposition from the question word**. The preposition must always come immediately *before* the "W" word:
*   **Από πού** τα αγόρασες; (*From where* did you buy them?)
*   **Με ποιον** μιλάς; (*With whom* are you speaking?)

---

## 5. Word Order & Intonation
To make a statement into a Yes/No question, you don't need to invert the subject and the verb like in English (e.g., *He is* -> *Is he?*). You often just change the punctuation to **( ; )** and use a questioning tone of voice.

*   *Statement:* Ο Γιώργος είναι εδώ. (George is here.)
*   *Question:* Ο Γιώργος είναι εδώ; (Is George here?)
