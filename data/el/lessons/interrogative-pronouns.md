# Unit 7: Asking Questions (Τι, Πού, Ποιος)

You now have a solid vocabulary and know how to conjugate verbs. The final step in your A1 foundation is learning how to ask for things. In Greek, this involves a special punctuation mark and learning the "W" words.

## 1. The Greek Question Mark (;)

The very first thing you must memorize is that the English question mark (?) **does not exist** in Greek. Instead, the Greek question mark looks exactly like the English semicolon (;). Be careful not to confuse the two when reading!

- **Πώς είσαι;** (How are you?)
- **Τι κάνεις;** (What are you doing?)

---

## 2. The Fixed "W" Words

These question words are easy because they are solid blocks. They never change their shape regardless of what you are asking about. Memorize these five:

- **Τι** (ti) = What? *(Τι είναι αυτό;)*
- **Πού** (poo) = Where? *(Πού είσαι;)*
- **Πότε** (pó-te) = When? *(Πότε φεύγουμε;)*
- **Πώς** (pos) = How? *(Πώς είσαι;)*
- **Γιατί** (ya-tí) = Why? *(Γιατί λες όχι;)*

> [!TIP]
> **The Secret of the Accents**  
> You learned in Unit 1 that 1-syllable words don't get accent marks. So why do **Πού** and **Πώς** have them? They keep the accent only to differentiate them from their identical twins: **που** (the relative pronoun "that/which") and **πως** (the conjunction "that"). This is why **'Τι'** doesn't have an accent—it has no twin!

---

## 3. The Chameleon "W" Words 

Just like adjectives (Unit 6), some question words are "chameleons." Since they ask about specific people or things, they must perfectly match the gender, number, and case of the noun you are hunting for.

### "Who" or "Which" (Ποιος)
Pronounced as a single snappy syllable (*pyos*). It acts exactly like a regular adjective.
- **Ποιος** είναι ο άντρας; (Who is the man?)
- **Ποια** είναι η γυναίκα; (Who is the woman?)
- **Ποιο** παιδί; (Which child?)

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **"Whom" do you see? (The Accusative Alert)**  
> If "Who" is the target of the action (the Object), it drops its **-ς** and adds a **-ν**, becoming **Ποιον**. 
> - *Ποιον βλέπεις;* (Whom do you see?)

### "How Much / How Many" (Πόσος)
This word also changes shape depending on what you are counting.
- **Πόσο** κάνει; (How much does it cost? - *Neuter singular*)
- **Πόση** ζάχαρη θέλεις; (How much sugar do you want? - *Feminine singular*)
- **Πόσα** παιδιά έχεις; (How many children do you have? - *Neuter plural*)

---

## 4. The "Preposition First" Rule

In English, it's very common to end a question with a preposition (e.g., "Where did you buy those shoes *from*?" or "Who are you talking *to*?"). 

**In Greek, you can NEVER separate the preposition from the question word.** The preposition must always strictly come *first*:

- **Από πού** είσαι; (*From where* are you?)
- **Με ποιον** μιλάς; (*With whom* are you speaking?)

---

## 5. Master the Intonation

To turn a normal sentence into a Yes/No question, you don't need to flip the word order or use helper words like "Do" or "Is" (e.g., *He is* -> *Is he?*). 

You simply keep the exact same word order, slap a Greek question mark **( ; )** at the end, and raise the pitch of your voice on the final word!

- *Statement:* Ο Γιώργος είναι εδώ. (George is here.)
- *Question:* Ο Γιώργος είναι εδώ; (Is George here?)
