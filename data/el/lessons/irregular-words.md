# Irregular & Defective Words 

You have mastered the rules of Greek grammar. You know how to decline nouns, conjugate verbs, and build complex sentences. 

But every language has its rebels. In Greek, there are words that flat-out refuse to follow the rules, and words that are "defective" (missing pieces!).

At Level C2, you need to know about these eccentrics. 

---

## 1. Nouns with Multiple Personalities (Double Plurals)

Some nouns in Greek are completely schizophrenic. They change gender when they become plural, or they offer you two entirely different plural forms depending on what you mean!

### The Gender-Benders
There are several highly common masculine nouns (ending in -ος) that suddenly decide to become **Neuter** (ending in -α) when they turn plural. 

- Ο χρόνος (the year) ➔ **Τα χρόνια** (the years). 
*(Never say "οι χρόνοι" unless you are talking about grammar tenses!)*
- Ο λόγος (the reason/word) ➔ **Τα λόγια** (the words).
*(Though "οι λόγοι" is used for "the reasons").*

### The "A-La-Carte" Plurals
The word for "Fox" (η αλεπού) is an old borrowing. Because it ends in a weird vowel, it couldn't decide how to be plural. So it has two accepted plurals:
- Η αλεπού ➔ οι αλεπ**ούδες** OR οι αλεπ**ούς**.

---

## 2. Nouns that Refuse to Decline (Indeclinable)

When Greek borrows words from English or French, it tries to fit them into the Greek system (e.g., Το σάντουιτς). 

But because they don't have Greek endings (-ος, -η, -ο), they are completely frozen. They are **indeclinable**. 
They rely entirely on the **Article** to show their case and number!

- **Το** σπορ (The sport - Nominative Singular)
- Βλέπω **τα** σπορ (I see the sports - Accusative Plural)
- Οι κανόνες **των** σπορ (The rules of the sports - Genitive Plural)

Notice that the word **σπορ** never changed!

---

## 3. Defective Verbs (Missing Tenses)

A "defective" verb is a verb that is missing an arm or a leg. It doesn't have a full set of tense conjugations. It only exists in specific forms.

We already saw the most famous defective verbs in Unit 27: **Impersonal Verbs**. 
Words like **πρέπει** (must) and **πρόκειται** (is about to) only exist in the 3rd person singular. You cannot say "εγώ πρέπω" (I must). It doesn't exist!

### The "Past Only" Ghost
There is a very cool, ancient verb that only exists in the Imperfect (Past Continuous) tense. It has no present, no future, and no simple past.

**Pre-existed / Was (Ήμουν / Υπήρχα)**
In C2 narrative writing or formal speech, you will often hear:
- **Επρόκειτο** (It was about to / It was scheduled to).
*(This is the past tense of Πρόκειται. But you cannot make it Future or Simple Past!)*

---

## 4. The Verb "Είδα" (I saw / I tell you!)

You know the Aorist verb **είδα** (I saw). 

But in spoken Greek, the command form **Ιδού** (Behold / Here it is) is a defective remnant from Ancient Greek. It is only ever used as this one frozen word.

- **Ιδού** η Ρόδος, ιδού και το πήδημα! 
*(Behold Rhodes, behold the jump - A famous ancient proverb meaning: "Stop bragging and prove it right here, right now!").*
