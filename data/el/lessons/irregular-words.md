# Irregular & Defective Words

You have mastered the rules of Greek grammar. You know how to decline nouns, conjugate verbs, and build complex sentences. But every language has its rebels—words that refuse to follow the rules, and words that are "defective" (missing pieces!).

---

## 1. Nouns with Multiple Personalities (Double Plurals)
Some nouns change gender when they become plural, or offer two different plural forms with distinct meanings.

### The Gender-Benders (Masculine to Neuter)
Several common masculine nouns ending in **-ος** become Neuter ending in **-α** in the plural. Often, both plural forms exist but mean different things:

*   **Ο χρόνος** (year/time) ➔ **Τα χρόνια** (years). *(Use "οι χρόνοι" only for grammar tenses!)*.
*   **Ο λόγος** (reason/word) ➔ **Τα λόγια** (the words). *("Οι λόγοι" = the reasons/speeches)*.
*   **Ο αδελφός** (brother) ➔ **Τα αδέλφια** (siblings). *("Οι αδελφοί" = specifically brothers)*.
*   **Ο δεσμός** (bond) ➔ **Οι δεσμοί** (relationships) vs. **Τα δεσμά** (literal shackles).

### The "A-La-Carte" Plurals (-ούδες)
Loanwords and words ending in stressed vowels often add an extra syllable in the plural:
*   **Η αλεπού** (fox) ➔ **οι αλεπούδες**.
*   **Η μαϊμού** (monkey) ➔ **οι μαϊμούδες**.
*   **Η γλωσσού** (gossip) ➔ **οι γλωσσούδες**.

---

## 2. Nouns that Refuse to Decline (Indeclinables)
Foreign loanwords often remain frozen. They rely entirely on the **Article** to show case and number.

*   **Το σπορ** (The sport) ➔ Βλέπω **τα σπορ** (I see the sports).
*   **Η τζαζ** (jazz), **η ελίτ** (the elite), **το ασανσέρ** (the lift).
*   **Common Gender:** ο/η ρεπόρτερ, ο/η μάνατζερ.

> [!NOTE]
> Even the letters of the Greek alphabet are neuter indeclinable nouns, like **το άλφα** and **το βήτα**!

---

## 3. Defective Verbs (Missing Tenses)
A "defective" verb (ελλειπτικό ρήμα) exists exclusively in the **imperfective aspect**—it lacks a Simple Past (Aorist) or Simple Future.

*   **ανήκω** (I belong).
*   **οφείλω** (I owe / I am obliged).
*   **τρέμω** (I tremble).
*   **ξέρω** (I know) - Has the imperfect **ήξερα**, but no Simple Past!

### Impersonal Ghosts
*   **Πρέπει** (must) and **Πρόκειται** (is about to) are frozen in the 3rd person singular.
*   **Επρόκειτο:** The formal past (Imperfect) of *Πρόκειται*.

---

## 4. The Verb "Είδα" & Ancient Remnants
While **είδα** (I saw) is a standard irregular verb, its ancient command form **Ιδού** (Behold) survives as a frozen remnant.

> [!TIP]
> **Famous Proverb:**  
> *"Ιδού η Ρόδος, ιδού και το πήδημα!"*  
> (Behold Rhodes, behold the jump!) — meaning: "Prove it right here, right now!"
