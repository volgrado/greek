# Masculine Declension (Κλίση Αρσενικών)

Let's look at the "cheat codes" for Masculine nouns. 

Almost all masculine nouns end in **-ος**, **-ας**, or **-ης**. These are the three families of masculine words.

Remember from our lesson on Cases: you only need to worry about the **Nominative** (Subject) and the **Accusative** (Object) for now. The Genitive (Possession) is included in the tables below for future reference.

---

## 1. Nouns ending in -ος

This is the biggest and most famous family of masculine words.
Example: **ο φίλος** (the friend)

| Case | Singular | English |
|---|---|---|
| **Nom** (Subject) | ο φίλ**ος** | *The friend* is here. |
| **Acc** (Object) | το(ν) φίλ**ο** | I see *the friend*. |
| Gen (Owner) | του φίλ**ου** | The car *of the friend*. |

**What happens?**
When the friend becomes the object (Accusative), two things happen:
1. The article **ο** changes to **τον**.
2. The noun **φίλος** drops its final **-ς** and becomes **φίλο**.

*Wait, why is it "το**ν**" sometimes and "το" other times?*
This is a pronunciation rule. You keep the 'N' if the next word starts with a vowel or a hard consonant (`κ, π, τ, μπ, ντ, γκ, τσ, τζ, ξ, ψ`). Otherwise, you drop the 'N'. Don't worry about memorizing this yet!

---

## 2. Nouns ending in -ας

Example: **ο πατέρας** (the father)

| Case | Singular |
|---|---|
| **Nom** (Subject) | ο πατέρ**ας** |
| **Acc** (Object) | το(ν) πατέρ**α** |
| Gen (Owner) | του πατέρ**α** |

**What happens?**
It's the exact same rule! The article becomes **τον**, and the noun drops its final **-ς**.

> [!TIP]
> **The "S" Rule**
> The golden rule for almost all masculine nouns in the singular: **If it's the subject, it keeps the 'S'. If it's receiving the action (Object/Accusative) or if it's the owner (Genitive), it drops the 'S'.**

---

## 3. Nouns ending in -ης

Example: **ο χάρτης** (the map)

| Case | Singular |
|---|---|
| **Nom** (Subject) | ο χάρτ**ης** |
| **Acc** (Object) | το(ν) χάρτ**η** |
| Gen (Owner) | του χάρτ**η** |

**What happens?**
You guessed it! The article changes to **τον** and the word drops its **-ς**.

---

## The Plural Forms

When you want to talk about *multiple* friends/fathers/maps, the endings change again to show Plurality.

| Ending | Plural Nominative (Subjects) | Plural Accusative (Objects) |
|---|---|---|
| **-ος** | **οι** φίλ**οι** (the friends) | **τους** φίλ**ους** (the friends) |
| **-ας** | **οι** πατέρ**ες** (the fathers) | **τους** πατέρ**ες** (the fathers) |
| **-ης** | **οι** χάρτ**ες** (the maps) | **τους** χάρτ**ες** (the maps) |

> [!WARNING]
> Notice that for the `-ας` and `-ης` groups, the plural forms are exactly the same whether they are subjects or objects! 
> - **Οι** πατέρες είναι εδώ. (The fathers are here).
> - Βλέπω **τους** πατέρες. (I see the fathers).
> The only thing that changes is the article (**οι** ➔ **τους**).
