# Negation (Η Άρνηση)

You know how to say "I play" (**παίζω**). But what if you are tired and want to say "I *don't* play"?

In English, we have to invent a helper word ("do") just to make a verb negative (I do not play). 

In Greek, making a sentence negative is incredibly simple. You just drop a single word right in front of the verb.

That word is **δεν** *(then)*.

---

## 1. Using "Δεν" to Negate Verbs

To say "not," you just stick **δεν** before the verb. It's like putting up a stop sign.

- παίζω (I play) ➔ **δεν** παίζω (I don't play)
- τρέχεις (You run) ➔ **δεν** τρέχεις (You don't run)
- έχουμε (We have) ➔ **δεν** έχουμε (We don't have)
- είναι (He is) ➔ **δεν** είναι (He isn't)

You can do this with literally every verb in the Present tense. There are no exceptions!

> [!CAUTION]
> **Wait, I thought "No" was "Όχι"?**
> You probably learned that "yes" is **ναι** and "no" is **όχι**. 
> - You use **όχι** to answer a question ("Do you want coffee? No.")
> - You use **δεν** to negate a verb ("I do *not* want coffee.")
> 
> Never say "Όχι θέλω καφέ". That sounds like "No I want coffee." You must say "Δεν θέλω καφέ."

---

## 2. Pronunciation: The Disappearing 'N'

Just like the article **τον** and **την**, the word **δεν** has a special pronunciation rule.

When Greeks speak fast, they will often drop the 'N' at the end of **δεν** if the next word starts with a soft consonant. 

You don't need to try and copy this yet, but it's important to know so you understand what you are hearing!

- Δεν κάνω. ➔ The 'k' in κάνω is hard, so it stays **δεν** κάνω.
- Δεν μιλάω. ➔ The 'm' in μιλάω is soft, so Greeks will pronounce it as ***δε*** μιλάω.

When you are writing, you should always write it as **δεν** to be safe. 

---

## 3. The Negative Power Combo (Δεν + Weak Pronouns)

In the previous unit, you learned that Weak pronouns (like **σε** for "you" or **τον** for "him") must be glued directly before the verb. 

What happens when **δεν** wants to jump in front of the verb too? Who wins?

**The pronoun always stays glued to the verb.** 

The negation word **δεν** comes *before* the pronoun.

::: example-box 
**Positive Sentence:** 
**Σε** βλέπω. (I see you).

**Negative Sentence:** 
**Δεν σε** βλέπω. (I don't see you).
::: end

::: example-box 
**Positive Sentence:** 
Ο Γιώργος **την** αγαπάει. (George loves her).

**Negative Sentence:** 
Ο Γιώργος **δεν την** αγαπάει. (George doesn't love her).
::: end
