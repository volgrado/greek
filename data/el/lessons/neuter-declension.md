# Neuter Declension (Κλίση Ουδετέρων)

Neuter nouns are the absolute easiest words to use in the Greek language. 

Why? Because **their Nominative (Subject) and Accusative (Object) forms are exactly the same.** They never change!

Almost all neuter nouns end in **-ο**, **-ι**, or **-μα**.

---

## 1. Nouns ending in -ο 

Example: **το βιβλίο** (the book)

| Case | Singular | English |
|---|---|---|
| **Nom** (Subject) | **το** βιβλί**ο** | *The book* is here. |
| **Acc** (Object) | **το** βιβλί**ο** | I see *the book*. |
| Gen (Owner) | του βιβλί**ου** | The pages *of the book*. |

**What happens?**
Nothing! The article stays **το**, and the word stays **βιβλίο**. 

---

## 2. Nouns ending in -ι 

Example: **το παιδί** (the child)

| Case | Singular | English |
|---|---|---|
| **Nom** (Subject) | **το** παιδ**ί** | *The child* is here. |
| **Acc** (Object) | **το** παιδ**ί** | I see *the child*. |
| Gen (Owner) | του παιδ**ιού** | The toy *of the child*. |

**What happens?**
Again, absolutely nothing! 

---

## The Plural Forms

When you want to talk about *multiple* books or children, the endings change to show Plurality.

Here is the best part: just like in the singular, **neuter nouns in the plural behave exactly the same whether they are subjects or objects!**

| Ending | Plural Nominative (Subjects) | Plural Accusative (Objects) |
|---|---|---|
| **-ο** | **τα** βιβλί**α** (the books) | **τα** βιβλί**α** (the books) |
| **-ι** | **τα** παιδ**ιά** (the children) | **τα** παιδ**ιά** (the children) |

Look closely:
- **Τα βιβλία** είναι εδώ. (The books are here - Nominative).
- Βλέπω **τα βιβλία**. (I see the books - Accusative).
- **Τα παιδιά** παίζουν. (The children are playing - Nominative).
- Φωνάζω **τα παιδιά**. (I call the children - Accusative).

> [!TIP]
> **The Golden Rule for Neuter Nouns**
> In the singular, the article is always **το**.
> In the plural, the article is always **τα** AND the word almost always ends in **-α**.

---

## 3. The Special Case: Words in -μα

We said almost all neuter nouns end in -ο or -ι. There is a third, very important group: those ending in **-μα**.

Examples: 
- το πρόβλη**μα** (the problem)
- το μάθη**μα** (the lesson)

These are very easy in the singular (they don't change, just like the others). But in the plural, they grow an extra syllable: **-τα**.

| Case | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| **Nom** (Subject) | **το** πρόβλη**μα** | **τα** προβλή**ματα** |
| **Acc** (Object) | **το** πρόβλη**μα** | **τα** προβλή**ματα** |

---

## 🎯 Wrap-Up Checklist

- [ ] I know that neuter singular nouns never change between being the Subject or the Object. The article is always **το**.
- [ ] I know that the plural article for neuter nouns is always **τα**, and the noun itself almost always ends in **-α**.
- [ ] I know that words ending in **-μα** add an extra **-τα** in the plural (το πρόβλημα ➔ τα προβλήματα).
