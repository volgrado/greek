# True Passive Voice (Η Παθητική Φωνή)

In Unit 18, we met **Deponent Verbs** (Type C). These are verbs that look passive (they end in **-ομαι**) but have an active meaning ("I come", "I need").

Now it is time to look at the **True Passive Voice**. 

This is when you take a normal, active verb (like "I wash") and flip it around so that the subject is receiving the action ("I am washed").

---

## 1. Creating the Present Passive

How do you turn an Active verb into a Passive verb? 
You drop the active ending (**-ω**) and attach the passive ending (**-ομαι**)!

Let's use the verb **πλένω** (I wash). 

- **Active:** Πλένω το φλιτζάνι. (I wash the cup).
- **Passive:** Το φλιτζάνι **πλένεται**. (The cup is washed).

You already learned these endings in Unit 18! Let's review them:

| Pronoun | The Passive Ending | Example (πλένομαι - *I am washed*) |
|---|:---:|---|
| (Εγώ) | **-ομαι** | πλέν**ομαι** | 
| (Εσύ) | **-εσαι** | πλέν**εσαι** | 
| (Αυτό/ή/ό) | **-εται** | πλέν**εται** | 
| (Εμείς) | **-όμαστε** | πλεν**όμαστε** | 
| (Εσείς) | **-εστε** | πλέν**εστε** | 
| (Αυτοί/ές/ά) | **-ονται** | πλέν**ονται** | 

> [!TIP]
> **The Reflexive Trick**
> In Greek, the Passive Voice doesn't just mean an action is done *to* you by someone else. It is also used to show actions you do *to yourself* (Reflexive).
> - Πλένω το παιδί = I wash the child. (Active)
> - **Πλένομαι** = I wash *myself* / I take a bath. (Passive/Reflexive)
> - Σηκώνω το κουτί = I lift the box. (Active)
> - **Σηκώνομαι** = I lift *myself* / I stand up. (Passive/Reflexive)

## 2. Who did it? (The word "από")

When a sentence is passive ("The house was built"), you can choose to include the person who actually did the building ("The house was built **by** George").

In Greek, the word for "by" in a passive sentence is **από** (from). And as you learned in the last lesson, 'από' is always followed by the Accusative Case!

::: example-box 
**Το βιβλίο διαβάζεται από τον Γιώργο.**
(The book is read **by** George.)
::: end

## 3. Creating the Past Passive (Aorist)

Here is where things get completely new. 

You know how to make an Active verb past tense (γράφω ➔ **έγραψα**). 
But how do you say "It **was** written"? 

The Passive Aorist has its own unique set of rules and its own unique set of endings.

### Rule 1: The Theta (Θ)
The hallmark of the Passive Past is the letter **Θ** (Theta). The vast majority of passive past verbs have a 'θ' right before the ending. 

### Rule 2: The Endings
The endings for the Passive Aorist are: **-ηκα, -ηκες, -ηκε, -ήκαμε, -ήκατε, -ηκαν**.

Let's look at the verb **πλένω** (I wash) in the Passive Past (I was washed).
1. Drop the -ω. (πλέν-)
2. Add the Theta. (πλέν-θ... wait, 'νθ' is hard to say. The 'ν' drops out! ➔ **πλύθ-** *(Yes, sometimes the vowels change too!)*)
3. Add the Augment (έ-) and the new endings!

| Pronoun | The Past Passive Ending | Example (πλύθηκα - *I was washed*) |
|---|:---:|---|
| (Εγώ) | **-ηκα** | πλύθ**ηκα** | 
| (Εσύ) | **-ηκες** | πλύθ**ηκες** | 
| (Αυτό/ή/ό) | **-ηκε** | πλύθ**ηκε** | 
| (Εμείς) | **-ήκαμε** | πλυθ**ήκαμε** | 
| (Εσείς) | **-ήκατε** | πλυθ**ήκατε** | 
| (Αυτοί/ές/ά) | **-ηκαν** | πλύθ**ηκαν** | 

*(Note: These endings already start with an 'η' which is a vowel, so you only need to add an extra 'έ-' augment if the verb root itself is extremely short).*

::: example-box 
**Ο καφές φτιάχτηκε από τη Μαρία.**
(The coffee was made by Maria.)
::: end
