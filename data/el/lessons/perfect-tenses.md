# Unit 20: The Perfect Tenses (I have done)

If you made it through the Past tenses, you will find the Perfect tenses incredibly easy. Modern Greek builds these tenses almost exactly like English! 

You simply combine a **Helper Verb** ("To Have") with a **Frozen Action Word** (like "played" or "written").

## 1. The Engine: The Helper Verb (Έχω)

In the Perfect tenses, the main verb never changes its shape. All the hard work of showing *who* is doing the action and *when* is handled entirely by the helper verb **έχω**. 

Depending on the timeline you want, you just choose its Present or Past form:

| Person | Present Helper (I have...) | Past Helper (I had...) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **(Εγώ)** | έχω | είχα |
| **(Εσύ)** | έχεις | είχες |
| **(Αυτός/ή/ό)** | έχει | είχε |
| **(Εμείς)** | έχουμε | είχαμε |
| **(Εσείς)** | έχετε | είχατε |
| **(Αυτοί/ές/ά)** | έχουν(ε) | είχαν(ε) |

## 2. The Frozen Word (Το Απαρέμφατο)

To create the action word (e.g., "played", "read", "written"), you take the **Simple Stem** (from Unit 12) and attach a specific ending. 

This special word is called the **Non-Finite** (*Απαρέμφατο*). It is completely frozen—it NEVER changes, regardless of who is doing the action!

### The Active Voice Ending: -ει
For regular active verbs, add the ending **-ει** to the active simple stem. The stress must always fall on the second-to-last syllable.
*   **διαβάζω** (read) ➔ διαβάσ- ➔ **διαβάσει**
*   **γράφω** (write) ➔ γράψ- ➔ **γράψει**
*   **ανοίγω** (open) ➔ ανοίξ- ➔ **ανοίξει**

### The Passive/Deponent Ending: -εί
To form the passive perfect, add **-εί** to the passive simple stem (the one that usually has a *θ, στ,* or *χτ*). **Notice that the stress loudly hits the final syllable!** 
*   **χάνομαι** (be lost) ➔ χαθ- ➔ **χαθεί** 
*   **κοιμάμαι** (sleep) ➔ κοιμηθ- ➔ **κοιμηθεί**

> [!WARNING]
> **The 1-Syllable Irregulars**  
> Do you remember the "Rebel" verbs from Unit 12? Since they shrink down to one syllable in the simple stem, their frozen word is also incredibly short!
> *   βλέπω ➔ **δει**
> *   λέω ➔ **πει**
> *   βρίσκω ➔ **βρει**

## 3. Assembling the Timeline

Now, just stick the Helper Verb and the Frozen Word together! 

### A. Present Perfect (I have done)
Used for an action completed in the past that matters *right now*.
*   **Έχω διαβάσει** το βιβλίο. (I *have read* the book).
*   **Έχεις κοιμηθεί;** (*Have you slept?*).

### B. Past Perfect / Pluperfect (I had done)
Used for an action completed long ago, usually before another past event. Use the past helper (**είχα**).
*   Όταν έφτασα, **είχε φύγει**. (When I arrived, *he had left*).
*   **Είχα χαθεί** χθες. (I *had been lost* yesterday).

### C. Future Perfect (I will have done)
Used for an action that will be fully completed before a specific deadline in the future. Just add the future particle (**θα**) to the Present Perfect!
*   **Θα έχω τελειώσει** μέχρι αύριο. (I *will have finished* by tomorrow).

## 4. The Pronoun Magnet

Weak object pronouns ("it", "him", "to me") love to cling to the front of the verb engine. In this case, the engine is **έχω**! They must completely jump over the frozen word and attach themselves to the front.

*   Το διαβάζω. ➔ **Το έχω διαβάσει.** (I have read *it*).
*   Της μιλάω. ➔ **Της είχε μιλήσει.** (He had spoken *to her*).
*   Δεν το κάνω. ➔ **Δεν το έχω κάνει.** (I haven't done *it*).

*(Notice how in a negative sentence, the pronoun safely hides between **δεν** and **έχω**).*
