# Unit 20: The Perfect Tenses (I have done)

In Greek, the Perfect tenses (Present Perfect, Past Perfect, Future Perfect) are built almost exactly like in English. You use a helper verb "**to have**" combined with a special, unchanging action word.

---

## 1. The Helper Verb: Έχω
You already know how to conjugate **έχω**. In this tense, it acts as the "motor" that drives the action.

- **Εγώ έχω** (I have)
- **Εσύ έχεις** (You have)
- **Αυτός έχει** (He has)
- **Εμείς έχουμε** (We have)
- **Εσείς έχετε** (You have)
- **Αυτοί έχουν** (They have)

---

## 2. The Frozen Word (-ει)
To create the action word (e.g., "played", "read", "written"), you take the **Simple Stem** (from Unit 12) and add the ending **-ει**. 

This word **never changes** regardless of the person.
- **παίζω** ➔ παίξ- ➔ **παίξει**
- **γράφω** ➔ γράψ- ➔ **γράψει**
- **διαβάζω** ➔ διαβάσ- ➔ **διαβάσει**

---

## 3. Assembling the Present Perfect
Simply put them together:
- **Έχω παίξει.** (I have played).
- **Έχουν διαβάσει.** (They have read).
- **Έχεις γράψει;** (Have you written?)

---

## 4. Past & Future Perfect
To change the timeline, you only change the helper verb **έχω**. The frozen word stays the same!

### Past Perfect (I had done)
Use the past tense helper **είχα**:
- **Είχα παίξει.** (I had played).

### Future Perfect (I will have done)
Use the future helper **θα έχω**:
- **Θα έχω παίξει** μέχρι αύριο. (I will have played by tomorrow).
