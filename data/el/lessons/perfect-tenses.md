# Unit 20: The Perfect Tenses (I have done)

In Greek, the Perfect tenses (Present Perfect, Past Perfect/Pluperfect, and Future Perfect) are built almost exactly like in English. You use an auxiliary (helper) verb "to have" combined with a special, unchanging action word. 

---

## 1. The Helper Verb: Έχω
You already know how to conjugate **έχω**. In these tenses, it acts as the "motor" that drives the action. Depending on the timeline, you will use its Present or Past (Imperfect) forms.

| Pronoun | Present (I have) | Past (I had) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Εγώ** | έχω | είχα |
| **Εσύ** | έχεις | είχες |
| **Αυτός/ή/ό** | έχει | είχε |
| **Εμείς** | έχουμε | είχαμε |
| **Εσείς** | έχετε | είχατε |
| **Αυτοί/ές/ά** | έχουν(ε) | είχαν(ε) |

---

## 2. The Frozen Word (The Non-finite / Απαρέμφατο)
To create the action word (e.g., "played", "read", "written"), you take the Simple Stem (from Unit 12) and add a specific ending. This special verb form is called the **non-finite** (απαρέμφατο). It is completely "frozen"—it never changes regardless of the person.

### Active Voice (-ει): 
For active verbs, add the ending **-ει** to the active simple stem. The stress always falls on the penultimate (last but one) syllable.
*   παίζω ➔ παίξ- ➔ **παίξει**
*   γράφω ➔ γράψ- ➔ **γράψει**
*   διαβάζω ➔ διαβάσ- ➔ **διαβάσει**

### Passive & Deponent Voice (-εί):
The perfect tenses exist in both active and passive voices. To form the passive non-finite, add **-εί** to the passive perfective stem (the one that usually has a *θ, στ, φτ,* or *χτ*). **Notice that the stress always falls on the final syllable!**. Deponent verbs (like *κοιμάμαι*) only have this passive form, even though their meaning is active.
*   χάνομαι (be lost) ➔ χαθ- ➔ **χαθεί**.
*   κοιμάμαι (sleep) ➔ κοιμηθ- ➔ **κοιμηθεί**.

> [!WARNING]
> **The 1-Syllable Irregulars**  
> The irregular verbs that have a 1-syllable dependent form (like *δω, πω, βρω*) create a 1-syllable non-finite word. 
> *   βρίσκω ➔ **βρει**
> *   βλέπω ➔ **δει**
> *   λέω ➔ **πει**

---

## 3. Assembling the Present Perfect
Simply put the helper verb and the frozen word together. It is used to express an action completed in the past that has relevance to the present.
*   **Έχω παίξει.** (I have played).
*   **Έχουν διαβάσει.** (They have read).
*   **Έχεις κοιμηθεί;** (Have you slept?).

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **The Pronoun Rule**  
> If you want to use weak object pronouns ("it", "him", "to me"), they **must** be placed immediately before the appropriate form of **έχω**. If the sentence is negative, they go right after **δεν**.
> *   **Το** έχω διαβάσει. (I have read *it*).
> *   **Δεν μας έχει** μιλήσει. ((S)he hasn't spoken *to us*).

---

## 4. Past & Future Perfect
To change the timeline, you only change the helper verb **έχω**. The frozen word stays exactly the same!

### Past Perfect / Pluperfect (I had done)
Use the past tense helper **είχα** to express an action completed before a specific time in the past.
*   **Είχα παίξει.** (I had played).
*   **Είχα χαθεί.** (I had been lost / I had lost myself).

### Future Perfect (I will have done)
Use the future particle **θα** + the present helper **έχω**. It expresses an action that will be completed by a particular point in the future. 
*   **Θα έχω παίξει** μέχρι αύριο. (I will have played by tomorrow).
*   *Pronoun reminder:* **Θα σου το** έχουν στείλει. (They will have sent *it to you*).
