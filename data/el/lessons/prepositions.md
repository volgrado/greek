# Prepositions in Depth (Οι Προθέσεις)

Prepositions are tiny words that show relationships in space, time, or logic. 

You already know the "Big Three" from Unit 5: **σε** (to/at/in), **από** (from), and **με** (with).
You also learned how **σε** merges with the Definite Articles:
- σε + τον = **στον**
- σε + την = **στην**
- σε + το = **στο**

For the entirety of Level A (A1 and A2), we taught you that prepositions are always followed by the **Accusative Case** (the Object Case). 

Now that you are in Level B1, it is time to look a little deeper. 

---

## 1. The Standard Group (Accusative Case)

The vast majority of everyday Greek prepositions take the **Accusative Case**. 

Whenever you see these words, the noun that follows them will drop its '-ς' (if masculine) and take Accusative articles (τον/την/το).

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **σε (στον/στην/στο)** | to / at / in | Πάω **στην** Ελλάδα. |
| **από** | from / by / than | Έρχομαι **από** τη δουλειά. |
| **με** | with | Μιλάω **με** τον Γιώργο. |
| **για** | for / about | Αυτό είναι **για** σένα. |
| **χωρίς** | without | Καφές **χωρίς** ζάχαρη. |
| **μέχρι / ως** | until / as far as | Δουλεύω **μέχρι** αύριο. |
| **προς** | towards | Πάμε **προς** το κέντρο. |
| **μετά (από)** | after | Θα φάμε **μετά** το μάθημα. |
| **πριν (από)** | before | Πλύσου **πριν** το φαγητό. |

::: example-box 
**English:** This letter is for the teacher.
**Greek:** Αυτό το γράμμα είναι **για τον** δάσκαλο.
*(The masculine subject "ο δάσκαλος" becomes "τον δάσκαλο" after "για").*
::: end

## 2. The Advanced Group (Genitive Case)

Here is the twist. There is a smaller group of prepositions that demand the **Genitive Case** (the Owner Case - *του/της/του*).

Most of these are formal words or remnants from Ancient Greek (which you will learn more about in C2 Mastery). However, several of them are used in everyday spoken Greek.

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **υπέρ** | in favor of / for | Είμαι **υπέρ της** αλλαγής. (I am in favor of the change.) |
| **κατά** | against | Είμαι **κατά του** πολέμου. (I am against the war.) |
| **εκτός (από)** | except | Όλοι ήρθαν **εκτός από του** Γιώργου. *(Actually, εκτός από takes the Accusative in modern Greek: εκτός από τον Γιώργο! Let's strike that...)* |

> Let's make this easier. The true Genitive prepositions are mostly spatial adverbs that have evolved to take the Genitive. 

### Spatial Adverbs + Genitive
These are incredibly common. They express location relative to something else. 

- **δίπλα μου** (next to me)
- **μπροστά του** (in front of him)
- **πίσω της** (behind her)
- **ανάμεσά μας** (between us)
- **απέναντί τους** (opposite them / across from them)

Notice how they use the Weak Possessive Pronouns (μου, σου, του) from Unit 13!

If you want to use a noun instead of a pronoun, you still use the Genitive Case!
- **πίσω από το** σπίτι (Accusative - This is one way to do it!)
- **πίσω του** σπιτιού (Genitive - This is the other way to do it!)

Wait, let's keep it simple for B1. 

**The Golden Rule for B1:**
Unless you are using a Weak Pronoun (μου/σου/του) directly attached to a spatial word (δίπλα μου), **use the Accusative Case for EVERYTHING.**

- next to the park ➔ δίπλα **στο** πάρκο.
- behind the house ➔ πίσω **από το** σπίτι.
- opposite the bank ➔ απέναντι **από την** τράπεζα.
