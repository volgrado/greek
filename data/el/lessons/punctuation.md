# Punctuation Marks
Greek punctuation is very similar to English, but there are two critical differences that often confuse beginners: the **question mark** and the **upper point**.

::: content-grid

::: content-card
.
### Full Stop (Τελεία)

Functions exactly as in English. Ends a sentence.

::: end

::: content-card
,
### Comma (Κόμμα)

Functions exactly as in English. Separates clauses.

::: end

::: content-card alert
;
### Question Mark (Ερωτηματικό)

In Greek, the semicolon character **is** the question mark.

::: example-box
Τι κάνεις; (How are you?)
::: end

::: end

::: content-card alert
·
### Ano Teleia (Άνω Τελεία)

The "Upper Point" functions like a colon or a semicolon in English. It is a dot placed at the top of the line.

::: end

::: end

## Comparison Summary

| Function | English | Greek |
|---|---|---|
| End of Sentence | . | . |
| **Question** | **?** | **;** |
| Pause/List | ; or : | · |

::: info-box mt-8 p-6 border-gray-200 border-accent bg-white/40
**Pro Tip:** When typing in Greek, the semicolon key (usually to the right of 'L') produces the Greek question mark **;**.

::: end
