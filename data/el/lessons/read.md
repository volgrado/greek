# Learn to Read: The "Phonetic Lego" System

Now that you know the Alphabet (Single sounds) and the Digraphs (Double Sounds), you have all the tools you need to read 99% of modern Greek. 

Unlike English, where "tough", "though", and "through" all look similar but sound completely different, **Greek is highly phonetic**. Once you memorize the sound a letter or combination makes, it almost *never* changes. It is like assembling a Lego set.

## Syllabic Assembly

Greek words sound exactly like they are spelled. To read Greek, mentally break every word down into syllables, normally stopping after a vowel.

### Example 1: Easy Syllables
Let's build the word for "mother".

::: content-card
**μα** *(ma)* + **μά** *(ma)*  
➔ **μαμά** *(ma-má)* = mom
::: end

### Example 2: Mixing Single and Double Letters
Let's build the word for "sky". It contains the double vowel **ου** *(oo)*.

::: content-card
**ου** *(oo)* + **ρα** *(ra)* + **νός** *(nós)*  
➔ **ουρανός** *(oo-ra-nós)* = sky
::: end

### Example 3: The Danger Zone (False Friends)
Let's build the word for "water". It looks like the English word "vepo". Let's prove why it's not.
- **ν** = *n*
- **ε** = *e*
- **ρ** = *r* (Rolled R, not a P!)
- **ό** = *o*

::: content-card
**νε** *(ne)* + **ρό** *(ró)*  
➔ **νερό** *(ne-ró)* = water
::: end

> [!WARNING]
> If you struggle with reading early on, **read out loud**. Say every syllable deliberately: *ne... ro*. *oo... ra... nos*. It builds the mental bridge much faster than reading silently.

## The Accent Mark (Τονισμός)

Did you notice the little dash mark over the vowels in the examples above? (**μά**, **νός**, **ρό**). This is called the *tonos*. 

In English, you just have to "know" which syllable to stress. (e.g. "**re**cord" the noun vs "re**cord**" the verb). In Greek, **the accent mark tells you exactly where to put the stress.**

Every word with two or more syllables must have exactly one accent mark. 

### Practice: Following the Stress
Look at the word for "problem": **πρόβλημα** *(pró-vli-ma)*. 
The stress is on the first syllable (**ό**). 

Now look at word for "math": **μαθηματικά** *(ma-thi-ma-ti-ká)*. 
The stress is all the way on the very last syllable (**ά**).

2. **Β** makes a `[v]` sound
3. **Ρ** makes an `[r]` sound
