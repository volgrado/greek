### 🔍 Reading Analysis

In this chapter, we focus on **Nouns & Articles**. Greek has three genders, and each has its own article:

*   **Masculine (ο):** **Ο** δρόμος (the road), **ο** υπάλληλος (the employee), **ο** χάρτης (the map), **ο** χρόνος (the time).
*   **Feminine (η):** **Η** πόρτα (the door), **η** λάμπα (the lamp), **η** σημείωση (the note).
*   **Neuter (το):** **Το** ξενοδοχείο (the hotel), **το** κλειδί (the key), **το** δωμάτιο (the room), **το** μυστικό (the secret).

**Grammar Hint:** Notice how masculine nouns often end in **-ος** or **-ης**, feminine in **-α** or **-η**, and neuter in **-ο** or **-ι**. Identifying the gender is the first step to mastering the Greek case system!
