### 🔍 Reading Analysis

In this chapter, we use the **Accusative Case**. This is the case used for the direct object of a sentence (who/what is receiving the action).

| Gender | Nominative (Subject) | Accusative (Object) | Combined with "σε" (to/in) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Masculine | **ο** άντρας | **το**ν άντρα | **στο**ν άντρα |
| Feminine | **η** σκιά | **τη** σκιά | **στη** σκιά |
| Neuter | **το** άγαλμα | **το** άγαλμα | **στο** άγαλμα |

**Grammar Hint:** When "σε" (to/at/in) meets the article, they merge into one word!
- **σε + τον = στον**
- **σε + την = στη** (or στην)
- **σε + το = στο**

Notice how the neuter **το** stays the same, while masculine and feminine articles change slightly or lose/gain the final **-ν**.
