### 🔍 Reading Analysis

In Greek, just like in the singular, **Plural Nouns** and **Adjectives** must match in gender.

| Gender | Article (Plural) | Noun Ending | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Masculine | **οι** | **-οι** | **Οι** δρόμ**οι** (The roads) |
| Feminine | **οι** | **-ες** | **Οι** σημειώσ**εις** (The notes) |
| Neuter | **τα** | **-α** (or -ια) | **Τα** έγγραφ**α** (The documents) |

**Adjective Agreement (Plural):**
- Masculine: **πολλο-ί**, **επικίνδυνο-ι**
- Feminine: **παλι-ές**, **κίτριν-ες**
- Neuter: **πολλ-ά**, **κρυφ-ά**

**Grammar Hint:** Notice that the article **οι** is used for both Masculine and Feminine plural! However, the noun endings (**-οι** vs **-ες**) will usually tell you the gender. For neuter nouns, the article **τα** and the ending **-α** are the gold standard.
