# Unit 24: Reflexive Pronouns (Myself)

Sometimes in life, the person doing the action and the person receiving the action are the exact same person. In English, we use words ending in "-self" (myself, yourself). 

In Greek, you have three distinct tools to express this: the pronoun phrase **ο εαυτός μου**, a Passive verb, or the advanced prefix **αυτο-**.

## 1. The Core Phrase (Ο εαυτός μου)

The word **εαυτός** (self) is a normal masculine noun. To say "myself," you simply put the definite article in front of it and add a weak possessive pronoun at the end! 

| Person | Pronoun Phrase (Accusative) | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st (Singular)** | **τον εαυτό μου** | myself |
| **2nd (Singular)** | **τον εαυτό σου** | yourself |
| **3rd (Singular)** | **τον εαυτό του / της** | himself / herself |

> [!TIP]
> **Native Secret: The Singular Plural**  
> How do you say "ourselves" or "themselves"? While you *can* make the noun plural (τους εαυτούς μας), native speakers overwhelmingly prefer to leave the word "self" in the singular shape, even when talking about multiple people!
> - Σκέφτονται μόνο **τον εαυτό τους**. (They only think about *themselves* / *their self*).
> - Πρέπει να προσέχουμε **τον εαυτό μας**. (We must take care of *ourselves*).

---

## 2. When to Use It (Emphasis & Emotion)

Because **εαυτός** is a noun, it operates grammatically just like any other direct object in a sentence. It almost always appears in the Accusative case, or right after a preposition.

- **Αγαπώ τον εαυτό μου.** (I love myself).
- **Προσέχεις τον εαυτό σου;** (Do you take care of yourself?).
- Πρέπει να βρεις χρόνο **για τον εαυτό σου**. (You must find time *for yourself*).
- Έχει μεγάλη ιδέα **για τον εαυτό της**. (She has a very high opinion *of herself*).

---

## 3. Passive Verbs vs. Pronouns

Wait! In Unit 18, we learned that Passive Verbs ending in **-ομαι** are used to do things to yourself (like taking a bath). So, when do you use the Passive, and when do you use the **εαυτός** pronoun?

**1. The Passive Voice (-ομαι) is for Routine & Grooming:**
When referring to daily bodily routines (washing, shaving, dressing), Greek strictly uses the passive verb.
- **Ντύνομαι.** (I dress myself).
- **Χτενίζομαι.** (I comb my hair).

**2. The Pronoun Phrase is for Emotion, Damage, & Emphasis:**
When the action is psychological, damaging, or needs special emphasis, you use the active verb plus the **εαυτός** phrase. (You generally cannot say "αγαπιέμαι" or "βλάπτομαι" in this context).
- **Μισεί τον εαυτό του.** (He hates himself).
- **Καταστρέφεις τον εαυτό σου.** (You are destroying yourself).

---

## 4. The C1 Advanced "Αυτο-" Prefix

There is a final, highly elegant way to express reflexivity. It is heavily used in literature, the news, and academic speech. 

You can glue the prefix **αυτο-** directly onto the front of a verb or noun to indicate that the action is directed back at the subject!

- Η **αυτο**πεποίθηση (Self-confidence).
- Η **αυτο**κόλλητη (Self-adhesive / sticker).
- Ο άνθρωπος αυτός **αυτο**καταστράφηκε. (This man self-destructed / destroyed himself).
