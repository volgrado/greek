# Unit 24: Reflexive Pronouns (Myself)

Reflexive expressions in Greek are used to express a situation in which the subject of the sentence is both the actor of the verb and the patient (the receiver). 

There are three ways of achieving this in Greek: by the reflexive expression **ο εαυτός μου** (myself), by a passive verb form, or by the verbal prefix **αυτο-** attached to a passive verb.

---

## 1. The Formula (Ο εαυτός μου)
The word **εαυτός** (self) is a masculine noun. To say "myself," you simply add the weak possessive pronoun after it. 

| Person | Greek | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st (Singular)** | τον εαυτό μου | myself |
| **2nd (Singular)** | τον εαυτό σου | yourself |
| **3rd (Singular)** | τον εαυτό του / της | himself / herself |
| **1st (Plural)** | τους εαυτούς μας | ourselves |
| **2nd (Plural)** | τους εαυτούς σας | yourselves |
| **3rd (Plural)** | τους εαυτούς τους | themselves |

> [!TIP]
> **Native Secret: The Singular Plural**  
> You might logically think that "ourselves" or "themselves" should always use the plural form (*τους εαυτούς μας/τους*). However, the definite article and the noun **εαυτός** are normally in the singular even when the possessive pronoun is in the plural! 
*   **Μόνο τον εαυτό τους κοιτάζουν.** (They only look after themselves).

---

## 2. How to Use them
The definite article *ο* and the noun *εαυτός* appear most frequently in the Accusative case when the reflexive expression is the direct object of the verb.
*   **Αγαπώ τον εαυτό μου.** (I love myself).
*   **Προσέχεις τον εαυτό σου;** (Do you take care of yourself?).

The reflexive expression may also occur in a prepositional phrase.
*   **Πρέπει να έχεις εμπιστοσύνη μόνο στον εαυτό σου.** (You should only trust yourself).
*   **Η Μαρία έχει μεγάλη ιδέα για τον εαυτό της.** (Mary has a very high opinion of herself).

---

## 3. Reflexive Verbs vs. Pronouns
In Unit 18, you learned that Passive Verbs (-ομαι) are often used reflexively. The passive verb form expresses reflexivity for some verbs of grooming or bodily care, especially when the subject is human.
*   **Πρέπει να ντύνεσαι ζεστά τον χειμώνα.** (You must dress [yourself] warm in the winter).
*   **Πάλι χτενίζεσαι;** (Are you combing your hair again?).

**So when do you use the pronoun?**  
You use the pronoun phrase (*τον εαυτό μου*) for special emphasis, or with verbs that do not inherently imply bodily care or do not have a reflexive passive form:
*   **Βλάπτω τον εαυτό μου.** (I am hurting myself).

---

## 4. The Advanced "Αυτο-" Prefix
A few reflexives can be formed with the prefix **αυτο-** attached to the passive verb. This is a true mark of B2/C1 fluency and is often used in news and literature!
*   **Ο άνθρωπος αυτός αυτοκαταστράφηκε.** (This man destroyed himself).
