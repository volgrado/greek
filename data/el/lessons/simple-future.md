# The Simple Future (Ο Συνοπτικός Μέλλοντας)

In Level A2 (Unit 11), you learned the **Continuous Future**. You used it to talk about ongoing habits or repeated actions in the future ("I will *be playing* every day").

To make the Continuous Future, you took the Present tense verb and put the word **θα** in front of it: Θα παίζω.

But what if you don't want to play every day? What if you just want to play a single game of tennis tomorrow?

You need the **Simple Future Tense**.

---

## 1. The Simple Stem

Remember the rule of Aspect from Unit 15?
- The Continuous Aspect is built on the **Present Stem**.
- The Simple Aspect is built on the **Aorist Stem**.

Because playing a single game of tennis is a one-time "Polaroid snapshot" event, we must use the Aorist Stem!

In the last lesson, you learned how to find the Aorist Stem by changing the final consonant.
- παίζω (I play) ➔ **παίξ-**

---

## 2. Assembling the Simple Future

To build the Simple Future, we need three ingredients:
1. The future word: **Θα**
2. The Simple Stem: **παίξ-**
3. The Endings... wait, which endings do we use?

We are talking about the Future, not the Past! So we **CANNOT** use the Past tense endings (-α, -ες, -ε...). 

Instead, we use the easiest endings in the world: **the Present Tense endings you already know (-ω, -εις, -ει...)!**

Look at how beautifully it comes together:

| Pronoun | Component 1 | Component 2 | Component 3 | Final Result | English |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|---|---|
| (Εγώ) | Θα | παίξ- | **-ω** | **θα παίξω** | I will play |
| (Εσύ) | Θα | παίξ- | **-εις** | **θα παίξεις** | You will play |
| (Αυτό/ή/ό) | Θα | παίξ- | **-ει** | **θα παίξει** | He will play |
| (Εμείς) | Θα | παίξ- | **-ουμε** | **θα παίξουμε** | We will play |
| (Εσείς) | Θα | παίξ- | **-ετε** | **θα παίξετε** | You will play |
| (Αυτοί/ές/ά) | Θα | παίξ- | **-ουν(ε)** | **θα παίξουν** | They will play |

> [!CAUTION]
> **No Augments allowed!**
> Remember the letter **ε** we added to the front of past tense verbs (έπαιζα)? 
> You MUST NOT add it to the Future tense! The augment **έ-** is exclusively a marker of the Past tense. The Simple Future only uses the pure stem (*παίξ-*).

---

## 3. Side-by-Side Comparison

Let's look at the two Futures side-by-side using the verb **γράφω** (I write).

- **Continuous Stem**: γράφ-
- **Simple Stem**: γράψ-

::: example-box 
**Scenario A: The Author**
Θα **γράφω** ένα βιβλίο κάθε χρόνο.
(I will *be writing* a book every year.)
*Repeated habit ➔ Continuous Stem.*
::: end

::: example-box 
**Scenario B: The Student**
Θα **γράψω** τις ασκήσεις μου αύριο.
(I will *write* my exercises tomorrow.)
*One-time completed action ➔ Simple Stem.*
::: end

It's that easy! Just swap the letter 'φ' for the letter 'ψ', and you have completely changed the aspect of the sentence.
