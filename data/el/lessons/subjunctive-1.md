# Unit 10: Verbal Aspect & The Subjunctive (Να)

Welcome to the great divide in the Greek language. To move from A1 to A2, you must understand **Aspect**. Greek doesn’t just care about *when* something happens (Tense); it deeply cares about *how* it happens (Aspect). 

---

## 1. The "Video vs. Photo" Concept (Aspect)
Greek forces you to choose between two ways of looking at an action:

*   **Continuous (Imperfective) Aspect — The Video:** Used for habits, routines, or ongoing background actions that are in progress or repeated. (e.g., "I am playing", "I play every day").
*   **Simple (Perfective) Aspect — The Photo:** Used for a single, specific, or completed event viewed in its totality. (e.g., "I want to play *one* game").

Because of this, every active Greek verb essentially has two stems:
*   **Continuous Stem:** The one you already know from the Present tense (e.g., **παίζ-**).
*   **Simple Stem:** The "Perfective" stem (e.g., **παίξ-**). We will learn exactly how to build this in the next units, but you will start seeing it everywhere now!

---

## 2. The Subjunctive Mood (Magic words: Να & Ας)
Greek has no "infinitive" form of verbs (like the English "to go" or "to play"). Instead, it uses the subjunctive mood, formed by the particle **να** followed by a conjugated verb. 

| English | Greek (Literal translation) | Greek (Actual) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| I want **to go**. | I want *that I go*. | Θέλω **να πάω**. |
| You want **to play**. | You want *that you play*. | Θέλεις **να παίξεις**; |

### When to use Να:
*   **After Verbs of Wishing, Ability, & Obligation:** This is the most common use. You use it after verbs like **θέλω** (I want), **μπορώ** (I can), and the impersonal **πρέπει** (I must).
*   **Deliberative Questions:** To ask for instructions or offer something. *Να σε ρωτήσω κάτι;* (May I ask you something? / Should I ask you something?).
*   **Commands & Wishes:** **Να** προσέχεις! (Take care / You should be careful!).

> [!TIP]
> **Bonus Magic Word: Ας (Let's / Let)**  
> While *να* is the standard subjunctive particle, Greek also uses **ας** to express suggestions, permission, or indifference. 
> *   *Ας φύγει.* (Let him go.)
> *   *Ας αφήσουμε αυτό το θέμα.* (Let's leave this subject.)

> [!WARNING]
> **The Subjunctive Negation Rule (Μη vs. Δεν)**  
> This is a huge rule! Indicative verbs (normal statements) are negated with **δεν** (I *do not* want = *δεν* θέλω). However, any verb introduced by **να** or **ας** MUST be negated by **μη(ν)**.
> *   Θέλω **να μην** έρθει. (I want him *not* to come.)
> *   **Να μην** το πείτε σε κανέναν. (You shouldn't tell anybody.)
> *(Note: μη(ν) follows the exact same "dropping 'N'" pronunciation rules you learned in Unit 5!)*

---

## 3. Aspect in the Subjunctive
Since **να** is followed by a verb, you must choose which "Aspect" (Video or Photo) to use for that verb:

*   **Θέλω να παίζω** *(Continuous/Video)*: I want to *be playing* (regularly, as a hobby, or continuously).
*   **Θέλω να παίξω** *(Simple/Photo)*: I want to *play* (this specific game, right now, just once).

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **The Secret of "Simple"**  
> Most of the time, when you ask for something, want to do something, or say you must do something, you are talking about a specific, single event. Therefore, **the Simple (Perfective) Stem is by far the most common one used with να**!
