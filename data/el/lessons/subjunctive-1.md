# Unit 10: Verbal Aspect & The Subjunctive (Να)

Welcome to the great divide in the Greek language. To move from A1 to A2, you must understand **Aspect**. Greek doesn’t just care about *when* something happens; it cares about *how* it happens.

---

## 1. The "Video vs. Photo" Concept
Greek forces you to choose between two ways of looking at an action:

1. **Continuous Aspect (The Video)**: For habits, routines, or ongoing background actions. (e.g., "I am playing", "I play every day").
2. **Simple Aspect (The Photo)**: For a single, specific, or completed event. (e.g., "I want to play *one game*").

Because of this, every active Greek verb has **two stems**:
- **Continuous Stem**: The one you know (παίζ-).
- **Simple Stem**: The "Aorist" stem (παίξ-). *We will learn how to build this in the next units.*

---

## 2. The Subjunctive Mood (Magic word: Να)
Greek has no "infinitive" (like "to go"). Instead, it uses **να** + a conjugated verb.  

| English | Greek (Literal) | Greek (Actual) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| I want **to go**. | I want **that I go**. | Θέλω **να** πάω. |
| You want **to play**. | You want **that you play**. | Θέλεις **να** παίζεις; |

### When to use Να:
- **θα + να**: "Θέλω να", "Μπορώ να", "Πρέπει να" (I must).
- **Commands**: **Να** προσέχεις! (Be careful!).
- **Wishes**: **Να** είσαι καλά. (Be well).

---

## 3. Aspect in the Subjunctive
Since **να** is followed by a verb, you must choose the aspect:

- **Θέλω να παίζω** (Continuous): I want to be playing (regularly, as a hobby).
- **Θέλω να παίξω** (Simple): I want to play (this specific game right now).

> [!TIP]
> **The Secret of "Simple"**  
> Most of the time, when you ask for something or want to do something, you are talking about a specific event. Therefore, the **Simple Stem** is the most common one used with *να*.
