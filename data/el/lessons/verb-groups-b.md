# Type B Verbs (The Accented Omegas)

In Unit 4, you mastered **Type A verbs** (like *διαβάζω* and *κάνω*). You learned that they are easy to spot because their final **-ω** does not have an accent mark on it.

Now it is time to meet the second major team of verbs verbs: **Type B**.

You know you are dealing with a Type B verb when the dictionary form ends in an Omega that **has an accent mark on it** (**-ώ**). 

---

## 1. Why are they different?

Type B verbs are rebels. When you conjugate them, they don't just add a regular ending (like *-εις* or *-ει*). They sneak an extra vowel (usually an **α** or an **ου**) right before the ending. 

Because of this, Type B verbs are subdivided into two families: **B1** and **B2**.

- **B1 verbs** love the letter **α**. 
- **B2 verbs** love the sound **ου**.

---

## 2. The B1 Family (The "Alpha" Lovers)

This is the biggest group of Type B verbs. 

These verbs often have an alternate dictionary spelling ending in **-άω**. If you see a verb ending in **-άω**, it is 100% guaranteed to be a B1 verb!

**Common B1 Verbs:**
- αγαπώ / αγαπάω (I love)
- μιλώ / μιλάω (I speak)
- ρωτώ / ρωτάω (I ask)
- ξυπνώ / ξυπνάω (I wake up)

Let's look at the conjugation for **αγαπάω** (I love). Notice how the letter **α** dominates almost every ending!

| Pronoun | B1 Ending | Example (αγαπάω) |
|---|:---:|---|
| (Εγώ) | **-άω** (or -ώ) | αγαπ**άω** |
| (Εσύ) | **-άς** | αγαπ**άς** |
| (Αυτό/ή/ό) | **-άει** (or -ά) | αγαπ**άει** |
| (Εμείς) | **-άμε** | αγαπ**άμε** |
| (Εσείς) | **-άτε** | αγαπ**άτε** |
| (Αυτοί/ές/ά) | **-άνε** (or -ούν) | αγαπ**άνε** |

> [!TIP]
> **Formal vs. Informal**
> You'll notice some alternate endings in parentheses above (like *αγαπώ* instead of *αγαπάω*). 
> The **-άω / -άει / -άνε** endings are the modern, everyday, spoken formats. 
> The **-ώ / -ά / -ούν** endings are considered more formal and literary (Katharevousa remnants). 
> We highly recommend you stick to the **-άω** format! It is much easier to pronounce and remember: just think "Alpha"!

---

## 3. The B2 Family (The "Oo" Lovers)

B2 verbs are much less common than B1s, but they include some incredibly important words. 

Unlike B1s, B2 verbs **never** have an alternate "-άω" spelling. They only end in **-ώ**. 

**Common B2 Verbs:**
- μπορώ (I can)
- ζω (I live)
- τηλεφωνώ (I call)
- οδηγώ (I drive)

Let's look at the conjugation for **μπορώ** (I can). Notice how the sound **ου** (`[ee]`) dominates the plural endings!

| Pronoun | B2 Ending | Example (μπορώ) |
|---|:---:|---|
| (Εγώ) | **-ώ** | βορ**ώ** |
| (Εσύ) | **-είς** | βορ**είς** |
| (Αυτό/ή/ό) | **-εί** | βορ**εί** |
| (Εμείς) | **-ούμε** | βορ**ούμε** |
| (Εσείς) | **-είτε** | βορ**είτε** |
| (Αυτοί/ές/ά) | **-ούν(ε)** | βορ**ούν(ε)** |

Notice something? The B2 endings look exactly like the Type A endings, but with an **ε** or an **ου** aggressively jammed in front of them!
