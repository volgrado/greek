# Unit 9: Verb Groups B1 & B2 (The Accented Verbs)

In Unit 4, you learned **Type A** verbs (like *κάνω*), where the accent is on the stem. Now we meet **Type B**—verbs where the accent is on the very last letter (**-ώ**). 

These verbs are the "singers" of Greek; they have a distinct rhythm and are split into two "vowel teams".

---

## Team B1: The "Alpha" Lovers (-άω)
These are the most common Type B verbs. They love the letter **α**. 
*Examples: **αγαπώ (αγαπάω), μιλάω, ρωτάω, ξυπνάω***.

| Pronoun | Ending | Example: **μιλάω** (speak) |
| :--- | :---: | :--- |
| (Εγώ) | **-άω** | μιλ**άω** |
| (Εσύ) | **-άς** | μιλ**άς** |
| (Αυτός/ή/ό) | **-άει** | μιλ**άει** |
| (Εμείς) | **-άμε** | μιλ**άμε** |
| (Εσείς) | **-άτε** | μιλ**άτε** |
| (Αυτοί/ές/ά) | **-άνε** | μιλ**άνε** |

> [!TIP]
> **Spoken vs. Written**  
> You might see **αγαπώ** (Formal) and **αγαπάω** (Spoken). They are the same! For a natural Masterclass feel, we recommend the **-άω** pattern as it’s the most common in modern Greece.

---

## Team B2: The "Oo" Lovers (-ώ)
These verbs are less common but very important. They prefer the **-ούμε** sound in the plural.
*Examples: **μπορώ, ζω, τηλεφωνώ, οδηγώ***.

| Pronoun | Ending | Example: **μπορώ** (can) |
| :--- | :---: | :--- |
| (Εγώ) | **-ώ** | μπορ**ώ** |
| (Εσύ) | **-είς** | μπορ**είς** |
| (Αυτός/ή/ό) | **-εί** | μπορ**εί** |
| (Εμείς) | **-ούμε** | μπορ**ούμε** |
| (Εσείς) | **-είτε** | μπορ**είτε** |
| (Αυτοί/ές/ά) | **-ούν** | μπορ**ούν** |

---

## How to tell them apart?
- If a verb has an **-άω** version (like *μιλάω*), it's **B1**.
- If it ONLY ends in **-ώ** (like *μπορώ*), it's **B2**.
