# Groups of Verbs
In Greek, verbs are categorized into "Conjugations" (Συζυγίες) based on their ending in the Present Tense. Knowing which group a verb belongs to is essential for conjugating it correctly.

::: content-grid mt-8

::: content-card first

### First Conjugation

-ω (unaccented)Verbs ending in **-ω** where the accent is on the stem.

- τρέχω (I run)
- παίζω (I play)

::: end

::: content-card second

### Second Conjugation

-ώ (accented)Verbs ending in **-ώ** where the accent is on the last syllable.

- αγαπώ (I love)
- νικώ (I win)

::: end

::: content-card passive

### Middle/Passive

-όμαι / -άμαιVerbs ending in **-μαι**. These often have a reflexive or passive meaning.

- κάθομαι (I sit)
- κοιμάμαι (I sleep)

::: end

::: end

::: info-box mt-12

### Why the Accent Matters

The accent mark on the final **-ώ** tells you that the verb follows a different set of endings (often adding an extra -α- in the conjugation).

Example: 1st Group **τρέχ-εις** vs 2nd Group **αγαπ-άς**.

::: end
