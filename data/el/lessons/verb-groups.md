# Groups of Verbs (Οι Συζυγίες)

Now you know that a Greek verb is made of a Stem + an Ending.

But not all verbs have the exact same endings. Greek verbs are split into two major teams, or **Conjugations** (Συζυγίες). 

How do you know which team a verb is on? You look at where the **accent mark** is in the dictionary form (the "I" form).

---

## 1. Type A Verbs (The Unaccented Omegas)

These are the most common verbs in the Greek language. If the verb ends in an Omega (**-ω**), but the Omega *does not* have an accent mark on it, it is a Type A verb.

The accent mark is sitting over the stem instead.

- π**αί**ζω (I play)
- τρ**έ**χω (I run)
- κ**ά**νω (I do / I make)
- διαβ**ά**ζω (I read / I study)

Because these are the most fundamental verbs, we are going to spend this entire unit mastering the Type A endings.

---

## 2. Type B Verbs (The Accented Omegas)

If the verb ends in an Omega, and the Omega *does* have an accent mark on it, it is a Type B verb.

- αγαπ**ώ** (I love)
- μιλ**ώ** (I speak)
- ξυπν**ώ** (I wake up)
- τηλεφων**ώ** (I call)

Type B verbs have a slightly different set of endings (they often sneak an extra 'α' or 'ου' into the ending). We won't worry about them until Unit 9 (so you can relax for now!). 

> [!TIP]
> **Warning: The Alternates**
> Many Type B verbs have an alternate spelling where they end in **-άω** instead of **-ώ**. 
> For example: **αγαπώ** and **αγαπάω** mean exactly the same thing. 
> Whether you see *αγαπώ* or *αγαπάω*, it's still a Type B verb. See the accent mark? It's on the ending!

---

## 3. Type C Verbs (The Passive "Omai's")

There is a third, very weird group of verbs. These verbs don't end in -ω at all! 

They end in **-ομαι** *(o-me)*. 

- έρχ**ομαι** (I come)
- γίν**ομαι** (I become)
- κοιμ**άμαι** (I sleep)

These verbs are incredibly important, but they require a completely different set of endings and grammar rules. This is called the **Passive Voice** (or "Deponent Verbs"). We will dedicate a massive part of B1 (Unit 18) to conquering these.

For the rest of level A1, we are only going to focus on **Type A** verbs! 

---

## 🎯 Wrap-Up Checklist

Look at the verb: **διαβάζω** (I read). 
- Does it end in -ω? Yes.
- Is there an accent mark on the -ω? No.
- Then it is a **Type A** verb!

Look at the verb: **μπορώ** (I can).
- Does it end in -ω? Yes.
- Is there an accent mark on the -ω? Yes!
- Then it is a **Type B** verb!

Look at the verb: **εργάζομαι** (I work).
- Does it end in -ω? No.
- It ends in -ομαι. It belongs to the **Passive** group.

If you can sort those three verbs into their correct buckets, you are ready for the next lesson!
