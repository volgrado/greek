# C2 Vocabulary: Ancient Phrases in Modern Greek

Modern Greek is heavily sprinkled with direct survivals from Ancient Greek. These phrases are used in formal settings, the news, and even everyday conversation to sound educated or poetic.

## 1. Expressions of Time & Status
- **εν τω μεταξύ** *(en to me-tax-í)* = in the meantime / meanwhile
- **καταρχάς / καταρχήν** *(ka-tar-hás)* = first of all / to begin with
- **εκ των προτέρων** *(ek ton pro-té-ron)* = in advance (e.g. "thank you in advance")
- **εκ των υστέρων** *(ek ton ees-té-ron)* = in hindsight / after the fact
- **τουλάχιστον** *(too-lá-hees-ton)* = at least (Note: this is an ancient superlative!)

## 2. Expressions of Agreement & Condition
- **δόξα τω Θεώ** *(dóx-a to the-ó)* = thank God (literally: glory to God)
- **παρεμπιπτόντως** *(pa-rem-peep-tón-dos)* = by the way / incidentally
- **ως επί το πλείστον** *(os e-pí to plís-ton)* = for the most part / mostly
- **επί τούτου** *(e-pí toó-too)* = on purpose / intentionally 
- **λόγου χάρη** *(ló-goo há-ree)* = for example / "for the sake of the word"

## 3. Academic & Formal Nouns (The -σις / -ση group)
These feminine nouns originally ended in -σις in ancient Greek.
- **η λύση / λύσις** *(ee lí-see)* = the solution
- **η κρίση / κρίσις** *(ee krí-see)* = the crisis / judgment
- **η γνώση / γνώσις** *(ee gnó-see)* = the knowledge
- **η δήλωση / δήλωσις** *(ee dí-lo-see)* = the statement / declaration
