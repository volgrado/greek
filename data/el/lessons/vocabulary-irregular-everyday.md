# C2 Vocabulary: Irregular Nouns & Verbs in the Wild

To achieve true mastery, you must know the common words that completely break the standard grammatical rules you learned in A1-B2.

## 1. The "Ancient" Plurals (Irregular Neuter Nouns)
Some neuter everyday words take an extra syllable (-ακ-) when pluralized or put in the genitive.
- **το κρέας** *(to kré-as)* = the meat ➔ Plural: **τα κρέατα**
- **το τέρας** *(to té-ras)* = the monster ➔ Plural: **τα τέρατα**
- **το φως** *(to fos)* = the light ➔ Plural: **τα φώτα** / Genitive: **του φωτός**

## 2. Unchanging Foreign Words
These words, heavily used in daily life, never change their endings no matter the case or number. The only thing that changes is the article (ο, η, το)!
- **το σπορ** *(to spor)* = the sport ➔ **τα σπορ** (the sports)
- **το κομπιούτερ** *(to kom-byoó-ter)* = the computer ➔ **τα κομπιούτερ**
- **το μπαρ** *(to bar)* = the bar
- **το ταξί** *(to ta-xí)* = the taxi (Be careful! It looks Greek but is foreign and doesn't change to ταξιά!)

## 3. The Rebellious Adjectives
The adjective "much/many" and "big" are famously tricky to decline. Learn these key forms:
- **πολύς, πολλή, πολύ** *(po-lís)* = much / many
    - *Plural Forms:* **πολλοί** (masc), **πολλές** (fem), **πολλά** (neut)
- **μέγας, μεγάλη, μέγα** *(mé-gas)* = great / huge (from ancient Greek, now used alongside 'μεγάλος')

## 4. The 3rd Person "Frozen" Genitive Absolute
Sometimes, Greeks use ancient participle constructions that seem disconnected from the sentence (Genitive Absolute).
- **όντος** *(ón-dos)* = being / in reality (e.g. **όλων των πραγμάτων όντων...** = all things being...)
- **τρέχοντος (μήνα)** *(tré-hon-dos)* = of the current (month)
