# Vowels and Consonants

Now that you can read syllables, it is crucial to understand the difference between **Vowels** and **Consonants**. 

Why? Because in Greek, the accent mark (τονισμός) can **only** be placed on a vowel. If you don't know your vowels, you won't know where to put the accent mark!

---

## 1. The 7 Vowels (Φωνήεντα)

Greek has exactly seven vowels. However, because of the way the language evolved over thousands of years, these seven vowels only make **five distinct sounds**.

### The Spelling "Cheat Sheet"
You might be wondering: *"If Η, Ι, and Υ all sound exactly the same, how do I know which one to use when spelling?"* 

There are strict grammatical rules that govern this, which you will naturally absorb. But here is a quick "cheat sheet" that works 80% of the time:

1. **Ι (Iota)** is the "default" `[ee]`. Most root words inside the dictionary use it (e.g., φ**ί**λος = friend).
2. **Η (Eta)** is mostly used at the absolute *end* of feminine nouns (e.g., η αγάπ**η** = the love).
3. **Υ (Upsilon)** is the rarest and is mostly found in words derived from Ancient Greek (e.g., **υ**πολογιστής = computer).
4. **Ο (Omicron)** is the default `[o]`.
5. **Ω (Omega)** is mostly used at the absolute *end* of verbs (e.g., θέλ**ω** = I want).

## 2. The 17 Consonants (Σύμφωνα)

Everything else in the alphabet is a consonant. 

**Β, Γ, Δ, Ζ, Θ, Κ, Λ, Μ, Ν, Ξ, Π, Ρ, Σ, Τ, Φ, Χ, Ψ**

Unlike English, where "Y" can sometimes act as a vowel, Greek consonants are **always** consonants. They never carry the accent mark.

### Voiced vs. Voiceless Consonants
You don't need to memorize this right now, but it's helpful to know that Greek consonants are split into two teams: **Voiced** (your vocal cords vibrate) and **Voiceless** (just air).

- **Voiced**: β, γ, δ, ζ, λ, μ, ν, ρ
- **Voiceless**: θ, κ, ξ, π, σ, τ, φ, χ, ψ

> [!CAUTION]
> **Why does this matter?**
> Remember the special double letters **αυ** and **ευ** from Lesson 2? 
> - If they are followed by a **Voiceless** consonant, they sound like an "F" (αυτοκίνητο ➔ a**f**tokinito).
> - If they are followed by a **Voiced** consonant, they sound like a "V" (αύριο ➔ a**v**rio).
