# Vowels and Consonants
Understanding the classification of Greek letters into vowels and consonants is the final step before mastering the stress system.

## The 7 Vowels (Φωνήεντα)

Greek has seven vowels, although some represent the same sound due to historical phonetic evolution.

::: info-box mt-8
**Did you know?** In Modern Greek, η, ι, υ all make the exact same "ee" sound. This is called *iotacism*.

## The 17 Consonants (Σύμφωνα)

Consonants are divided into categories based on how they are produced (voiced vs. voiceless), but for now, remember their forms:

Β, Γ, Δ, Ζ, Θ, Κ, Λ, Μ, Ν, Ξ, Π, Ρ, Σ, Τ, Φ, Χ, Ψ
## The Importance of Vowels

Vowels are the carriers of the **accent**. In Lesson 5, you will learn that the accent mark can only be placed above one of these seven vowels.
