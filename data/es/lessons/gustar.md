# Gustar
The verb **gustar** doesn't mean 'to like' in the way English does. It literally means 'to be pleasing to.'

## The Structure

The thing being 'liked' is actually the subject of the sentence. Therefore, we mostly use only two forms: **gusta** (singular) and **gustan** (plural).

- Me gusta el café. (I like coffee (Coffee is pleasing to me).)
- Me gustan los libros. (I like books (Books are pleasing to me).)

### Indirect Object Pronouns

You must use a pronoun with gustar to show who is pleased:

**me, te, le, nos, os, les**

¿Te gusta la música?
