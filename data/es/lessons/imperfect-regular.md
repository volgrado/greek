# Imperfect: Regular Verbs
The imperfect tense is used to describe ongoing, habitual, or descriptive actions in the past (was doing, used to do).

## -ar Endings

| yo | -aba |
|---|---|
| tú | -abas |
| él/ella | -aba |
| nosotros | -ábamos |
| ellos/ellas | -aban |

## -er / -ir Endings

Just like in the preterite, -er and -ir verbs share the same endings in the imperfect.

| yo | -ía |
|---|---|
| tú | -ías |
| él/ella | -ía |
| nosotros | -íamos |
| ellos/ellas | -ían |

::: info-box

### Accent Alert!

In the imperfect, all -er/-ir endings have an accent mark on the 'i'.

::: end
