# Subjunctive Usage: Ojalá
The word **Ojalá** (meaning 'hopefully' or 'I wish to God') always triggers the subjunctive.

## Using Ojalá

You can use it with or without 'que'.

- ¡Ojalá llueva! (I hope it rains!)
- Ojalá que tú ganes. (Hopefully you win.)

::: info-box

### Origin

'Ojalá' comes from the Arabic 'Insha'Allah', reflecting the long historical influence of Arabic on the Spanish language.

::: end
