The Interjections

Interjections (Επιφωνήματα) are words that express sudden emotions or reactions. They are often used in spoken Greek to add color and feeling to a conversation.

  • Μπράβο!Bravo! / Well done!
  • Α μπά!No way! / Don't tell me!
  • Άντε! / Άιντε!Come on! / Let's go!
  • Αχ!Oh! / Ah! (pain or sigh)
  • Μακάρι!I wish! / Hopefully!
  • Αλίμονο!Alas! / Unfortunately!
  • Εύγε!Excellent! / Bravo!

Cultural Highlight: "Άντε"

Άντε is one of the most common words in Greece. Depending on the tone, it can mean "Come on", "Let's go", "Hurry up", or even "Get out of here!" (in disbelief).
