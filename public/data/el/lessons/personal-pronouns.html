Personal Pronouns

The personal pronouns in Greek change based on the person (1st, 2nd, 3rd) and their function in the sentence (Subject vs. Object).

1. Subject Pronouns (Nominative)

These are used to represent the person performing the action.

Person Singular Plural
1st (I / We) εγώ εμείς
2nd (You) εσύ εσείς
3rd (He/She/It) αυτός (M) αυτή (F) αυτό (N) αυτοί (M) αυτές (F) αυτά (N)

2. Weak vs. Strong Forms

Greek has two ways to say things like "me" or "him". Strong forms are for emphasis, while Weak forms (clitics) are used in normal speech and usually attached to the verb.

Example Strong (Emphatic) Weak (Normal)
Me εμένα με
You εσένα σε
Him αυτόν τον
Her αυτή(ν) τη(ν)

Usage in Sentences

  • Σε βλέπω. (I see you - Normal)
  • Βλέπω εσένα. (I see YOU - Emphasized)
