Intro to Pronouns

Pronouns are words used in place of nouns to avoid repetition and make speech more fluid. Modern Greek has several categories of pronouns, most of which decline based on gender, number, and case—just like the nouns they replace.

::: pronoun-categories mt-8

Personal Pronouns

I, you, he, she, it...

Possessive Pronouns

My, your, his, hers...

Demonstrative Pronouns

This, that, these, those...

Relative Pronouns

Who, which, that...

::: info-box mt-12

Key Rule: Omission

In Modern Greek, subject pronouns (I, you, we) are often omitted because the verb ending already tells us who is performing the action. We only use them for emphasis or clarity.

Διαβάζω. (I read) vs Εγώ διαβάζω. (I [emphasized] read) ::: end
