Verb Tenses

Greek verb tenses are divided based on when the action happens (Past, Present, Future) and how it happens (Continuous vs. Simple/Instant).

::: tenses-timeline mt-8

Present (Ενεστώτας)

παίζω I am playing / I play

Future (Μέλλοντας)

θα παίζω I will be playing (continuous)θα παίξω I will play (once)

Past (Παρελθόν)

έπαιζα I was playing (Imperfect)έπαιξα I played (Aorist)

Perfect (Παρακείμενος)

έχω παίξει I have played ::: end

::: info-box mt-12 mb-12

The Aspect Rule

In Greek, the most important distinction is between Continuous (actions that happen repeatedly or over time) and Simple (actions that happen once). This distinction is reflected in most tenses!
