Introduction to Verbs

In Ancient and Modern Greek, verbs are the heartbeat of the sentence. Most regular verbs follow predictable patterns. Explore the Present Indicative Active below.

The Anatomy of a Greek Verb

Greek verbs have a stem and an ending. The stem carries the meaning (e.g., λύ- means "loose"), while the ending tells us who is performing the action.

  • -ω: 1st Person Singular (I)
  • -εις: 2nd Person Singular (You)
  • -ει: 3rd Person Singular (He/She/It)
