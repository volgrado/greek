Comparisons of Inequality

In Spanish, we use specific formulas to compare things that are not equal (more than or less than).

More than / Less than

  • más + [adjective/noun] + que (more than)
  • menos + [adjective/noun] + que (less than)

Examples

  • Juan es más alto que Pedro. (Juan is taller than Pedro.)
  • Tengo menos dinero que tú. (I have less money than you.)

Irregular Comparatives

Some adjectives have their own comparative forms:

  • Bueno → Mejor (Better)
  • Malo → Peor (Worse)
  • Viejo → Mayor (Older)
  • Joven → Menor (Younger)
