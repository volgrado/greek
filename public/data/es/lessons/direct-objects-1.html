Direct Object Pronouns I

Direct object pronouns replace nouns that receive the action of a verb, helping to avoid repetition.

The Forms

Person Singular Plural
1st me (me) nos (us)
2nd te (you) os (you all)
3rd lo / la (it/him/her) los / las (them)

Placement Rule

In a simple sentence with one conjugated verb, the direct object pronoun must come before the verb.

Yo tengo el libro → Yo lo tengo.
