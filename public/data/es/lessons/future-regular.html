Future Tense: Regular

The future tense in Spanish is used to describe what will happen. Unlike most other tenses, the endings are added directly to the entire infinitive.

One Set of Endings

All regular -ar, -er, and -ir verbs use the same set of endings in the future tense.

yo -é
tú -ás
él/ella -á
nosotros -emos
ellos/ellas -án

Examples

  • Hablaré (I will speak)
  • Comerás (You will eat)
  • Viviremos (We will live)

Accent Alert!

Notice that all endings EXCEPT the 'nosotros' form have an accent mark.
