Imperfect Subjunctive III

The choice between present and imperfect subjunctive depends on the tense of the main clause.

The Sequence of Tenses

  • If the main verb is in Present, Future, or Imperative → Use Present Subjunctive.
  • If the main verb is in Preterite, Imperfect, or Conditional → Use Imperfect Subjunctive.

Examples

::: example-box Quiero que vengas. (I want you to come - Present)

Quería que vinieras. (I wanted you to come - Past)

Polite Requests

The imperfect subjunctive of 'querer' (quisiera) is frequently used to make very polite requests.

Quisiera un café, por favor.
