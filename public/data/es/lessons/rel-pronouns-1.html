Relative Pronouns I: Que

Relative pronouns are used to connect two sentences that share a common noun. Que is the most common relative pronoun in Spanish.

Using 'Que'

Unlike in English, where you can often omit 'that' (the book [that] I read), in Spanish you must always use 'que'.

  • 'Que' can refer to people or things.
  • It translates to: that, which, who, whom.

Examples

  • El libro que compré es bueno. (The book (that) I bought is good.)
  • La mujer que habla es mi tía. (The woman who is speaking is my aunt.)
