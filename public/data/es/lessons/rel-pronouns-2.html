Relative Pronouns II: Quien

While 'que' is the universal pronoun, quien (singular) and quienes (plural) are used specifically for people, usually after a preposition.

Using 'Quien'

Use 'quien' when referring to people after prepositions like a, con, de, para.

  • La mujer a quien llamaste no está. (The woman whom you called is not here.)
  • Los amigos con quienes viajo son simpáticos. (The friends with whom I travel are nice.)

Lo que

Use lo que to mean 'what' or 'that which' when referring to an abstract idea, situation, or a previous clause.

Lo que necesito es tiempo. (What I need is time).
