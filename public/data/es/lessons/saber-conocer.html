Saber vs Conocer

Both verbs mean 'to know,' but they are used in completely different contexts.

Saber

Used for facts, information, and skills (how to do something).

  • Yo sé la verdad.
  • Sé nadar.

Conocer

Used for people, places, and things (being familiar with).

  • Conozco a María.
  • Conozco Madrid.

Conjugation Check

Both are irregular in the yo form:

saber → sé | conocer → conozco
