Si Clauses II

Hypothetical or contrary-to-fact conditions require a specific combination of tenses.

Type 2: Improbable / Unlikely Conditions

If you are talking about something that is not currently true (If I were rich...), use the subjunctive.

Pattern: Si + Imperfect Subjunctive → Conditional

::: example-box Si fuera rico, compraría un coche. (If I were rich, I would buy a car.)

The Magic Mnemonic

A common way to remember this is: Subjunctive-Si, Conditional-Other.
