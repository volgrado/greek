Subjunctive: Irregular Yo

Because the subjunctive is based on the 'yo' form, verbs with irregular 'yo' forms in the present tense carry that irregularity into all subjunctive forms.

Verbs with -go, -zco, etc.

  • Tener (yo tengo) → tenga, tengas, tenga...
  • Hacer (yo hago) → haga, hagas, haga...
  • Conocer (yo conozco) → conozca, conozcas...
  • Poner (yo pongo) → ponga, pongas...

Why is this important?

This rule ensures that the 'hard' or 'soft' sound of the verb is preserved throughout the mood.
